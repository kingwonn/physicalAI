## 主要玩家的方案与批判性评价 (Players Solutions & a Critical Read)

> This section grades the on-device-brain players against the §K rubric (demand a SoC SKU + duty cycle for every Hz/TOPS; demand per-task-class OOD success + third-party pairwise eval; treat unpublished MTBF as the finding) and the §L reference stack (S2 7–10 Hz VLM / S1 100–200 Hz policy / S0 500 Hz–1 kHz reflex island). The honest read, up front: **almost every headline "fully autonomous on-device" claim in this cohort is company-reported, and the two most-marketed players (Tesla, Figure) have the weakest independent evidence.** Genuine, verifiable on-device inference exists — Figure Helix, Gemini Robotics On-Device, and (via Jetson Thor) GR00T/π0 — but it is *inference-local, teleop-trained*, not *autonomy-discovered*. Everything is dated **as of 2026-07**; flags are marked inline: `(company-reported)` = vendor's own claim/livestream, no third-party audit; `(teleop-assisted)` = confirmed or reported human-in-the-loop; `(marketing)` = press/launch framing; `(unverified)` = single-source or not found in a primary source. Cross-refs: [VLA models](vla-models.md), [Figure](company-figure.md), [NVIDIA](company-nvidia.md), [PI](company-pi.md), [Optimus](company-optimus.md), [AgiBot](company-agibot.md), [UBTech](company-ubtech.md), [Unitree](company-unitree.md), [ODM competitors](odm-competitors.md).

### 2. Critical profiles by camp

#### Western frontier

**Figure (Helix / Helix-02)** — [company-figure.md](company-figure.md)
- **Solution (as of 2026-01):** dual-system VLA — System 2 = 7B internet-pretrained VLM @7–9 Hz for scene/language; System 1 = 80M visuomotor transformer @**200 Hz** across 35 DoF (fingers/wrists/torso/head). Helix-02 adds an "System 0" whole-body net unifying locomotion+manipulation+balance. Runs on **dual low-power embedded GPUs onboard** under ~60 W with 4-bit quant + model-parallel split. Trained on ~500 h teleop demos (VLM auto-labeled).
- **Is it really on-device?** **Yes for inference** — no cloud round-trip claimed or reported for the control loop, a genuine differentiator vs π0's server-tethered default. This maps cleanly onto the §L stack (S2 7–9 Hz / S1 200 Hz / Helix-02 S0). **But three flags:** (1) the 200 Hz is achieved *at 80M params*, not a full VLA (§K Anchor 1) — impressive, not magic; (2) **chip ID (Jetson Orin) is secondary-blog-sourced, not Figure's own post (unverified)**; (3) the May-2026 24/7 warehouse livestream (3–4 robots, 50k–100k+ packages over 24–81 h, Adcock: "no teleoperation") is **Figure's own uncorroborated livestream** — TechRadar and others flagged that not everyone is convinced it was "fully real," and no third party has verified failure rates or environment control **(company-reported)**.
- **Strengths:** real edge inference at a usable 200 Hz control loop without cloud; single-weight-set policy across many manipulation tasks with zero task-specific fine-tune claimed; fast public cadence (Helix→Helix-02 in <1 yr).
- **Critical read:** the training-data lineage is **100% teleoperation**, so the "autonomy" is autonomous *imitation of teleop trajectories*, not independently-discovered policy. Against §K: no per-task-class OOD success table, no MTBF, no third-party (RoboArena-style) eval — every long-duration claim is self-reported. Classic self-reported-benchmark risk pattern; the engineering (onboard 200 Hz) is real, the *autonomy narrative* is unaudited.

**NVIDIA (GR00T N1.6 + Jetson Thor + Isaac)** — [company-nvidia.md](company-nvidia.md)
- **Solution:** explicitly a **platform, not a robot**: open foundation model (GR00T N1.5→N1.6, weights on HF) + data/sim pipeline (Isaac Lab, Cosmos world models) + CUDA-X runtime + Jetson AGX Thor T5000 (Blackwell, 2,070 FP4 TFLOPS, 128 GB, 40–130 W). June-2026: open "Isaac GR00T Reference Humanoid" design (Unitree-manufactured, late 2026). Licensees: Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, Franka, NEURA, Skild.
- **Is it really on-device?** Jetson Thor genuinely supports local inference (that's the product), and GR00T weights are open, so any deployment *can* be fully local (§I benchmarks GR00T-N1.6 at 10.9 Hz TensorRT / 22–24 Hz hand-CUDA on Thor). **But NVIDIA operates no flagship robot to audit** — the on-device-vs-cloud reality is inherited from whichever integrator ships it, making NVIDIA's own on-device claim *unfalsifiable as a single product*.
- **Strengths:** genuinely open weights + unusually complete full stack lowers the barrier for startups to get a working VLA without building chips or foundation models — real adoption (named OEMs, HF downloads) proves it's more than vaporware *as infrastructure*.
- **Critical read:** picks-and-shovels vendor with **no skin in any autonomy claim** — the "Android of robotics" framing means NVIDIA wins even if the robot-brains atop GR00T are teleoperated, unreliable, or narrow. The June-2026 reference humanoid is a spec built by a third party, not volume-shipping. Grade it as infrastructure (strong) not as an autonomous brain (N/A).

**Physical Intelligence (π0 / π0.5, openpi)** — [company-pi.md](company-pi.md)
- **Solution:** flow-matching VLA (π0, π0-FAST, π0.5); π0.5 pretrained 10k+ h with "knowledge insulation" for open-world generalization. **Fully open weights** via openpi (JAX/Flax + PyTorch), public GCS checkpoints — the most genuinely open release in the cohort. Documented GPU minimums: >8 GB VRAM inference (RTX 4090), >22.5 GB LoRA, >70 GB full FT (A100/H100).
- **Is it really on-device? This is the central skeptic finding.** PI's **own documented default is a policy *server*, not on-device** — the openpi README's reference workflow (`serve_policy.py` on port 8000, robot queries over websocket) means real robots in PI's own examples are tethered to a co-located/networked GPU. The independent VLA-Perf benchmark quantifies why it's tight: π0 ≈73 ms/inference on RTX 4090, ~53 ms best-case Thor estimate (§L/§I: π0 = 19.0 Hz on Thor). A true on-device path (Jetson Thor + TensorRT NVFP4) **exists but is an NVIDIA-authored tutorial, not PI's shipped default**.
- **Strengths:** most transparent, reproducible, fully open-weight offering — actual checkpoints + training code + documented HW minimums let outsiders independently verify latency/quality (unlike Figure/Tesla). Credible edge-migration story via Thor+quant.
- **Critical read:** the "VLA needs a GPU" catch is **real and self-documented** — many real-world π0 demos are edge-server hybrids, not phone-class on-device compute. It becomes "on-device" only with extra NVIDIA-side quantization layered on top. Honest grading: best *evaluability*, but not an out-of-box on-device brain.

**Google DeepMind (Gemini Robotics On-Device)**
- **Solution (announced 2025-06-25):** two tiers — cloud "Gemini Robotics" (full Gemini scale) + **"Gemini Robotics On-Device," a deliberately distilled VLA built to run locally with no network dependency**. Trained on ALOHA bi-arm, adapted to Franka FR3 + Apptronik Apollo; adapts with **50–100 demos**. Status **"Private preview" / "Trusted tester"** — not GA. No chip/SoC named; no open-weights claim (closed). Apollo partner backed by $520M Feb-2026 Series-A extension.
- **Is it really on-device?** **Most deliberately-honest edge framing in the cohort** — DeepMind built a *separate, smaller model specifically for local inference* (not cloud-Gemini-with-a-wrapper), framed around latency/bandwidth-constrained settings. **But** it stays in closed private preview; no broad field validation, no benchmark vs the cloud tier, no chip specs, no failure-rate data. Credible in *intent/design*, unverified at meaningful scale outside Google's trusted-tester circle (§I: the "7-task >60% OOD" figure could not be verified in the primary blog — marked **(unverified)**).
- **Strengths:** conceptually the cleanest on-device realization — ships a separate purpose-built small model rather than post-hoc quantizing the flagship; documented low-shot FT + cross-embodiment portability (ALOHA→Franka→Apollo) as public proof points.
- **Critical read:** classic well-resourced-lab pattern — previewing edge capability years before broad shipping. Real reliability, hardware envelope, and true parity vs the cloud flagship remain undisclosed and effectively unverifiable from outside. High design credibility, zero independent field data.

**Tesla (Optimus, AI5 silicon)** — [company-optimus.md](company-optimus.md)
- **Solution:** vision-only, end-to-end NN stack shared with FSD (no LiDAR/radar) — per VP Ashok Elluswamy (Feb-2026 ScaledML), extends the ~48-net FSD architecture with locomotion/manipulation/balance nets + a Grok voice layer, perception representations shared car↔robot. **Custom silicon: AI5 taped out 2026** (~5× usable / ~8× raw compute vs AI4, dual-sourced TSMC AZ + Samsung TX; first samples late 2026, volume mid-2027). Optimus V3 (22-DoF hands, AI5, Grok) targeted low-volume Fremont summer 2026. **Fully closed** — no open weights, no Helix/GR00T/openpi-equivalent paper.
- **Is it really on-device? The skeptic case is the strongest and best-documented of all six frontier players.** We-Robot 2024: Optimus units **confirmed remotely operated** (TechCrunch, Bloomberg) — one told a guest on camera it was "assisted by a human"; Morgan Stanley's Adam Jonas called it "not operating entirely autonomously." Tesla-Diner July-2025 popcorn demo: **confirmed remote-controlled**, undisputed by Musk. A later Oct-2025 kung-fu video Musk *claimed* was AI-driven — **his own unverified assertion**, not audited **(teleop-assisted / unverified)**. On the Q4-2025 call (Jan-28-2026) **Musk himself called current Optimus units "primarily for learning, not productive tasks"** — i.e., still R&D-stage. Chinese sourcing corroborates reliance on remote operation.
- **Strengths:** real, uniquely vertically-integrated custom-silicon program — **AI5 tape-out is a concrete, verifiable hardware milestone** (one of very few players taping out custom edge AI silicon vs buying merchant GPUs); reusing the FSD vision-only stack across cars+robots is a genuine efficiency others lack.
- **Critical read:** **highest vaporware/teleop risk in the cohort by direct evidence** — flagship demos confirmed teleoperated and *not proactively disclosed*, only surfaced via press/on-camera admissions; Musk's own Jan-2026 "for learning, not productive tasks" undercuts years of onstage productivity claims; the chip meant to enable the real on-device brain (AI5) **doesn't exist in volume until mid-2027**, so current Optimus necessarily runs on weaker silicon or stays teleop-dependent. Rodney Brooks (2025): Musk's Optimus vision is "pure fantasy thinking."

**Skild AI (Skild Brain, omni-bodied)** — [company-skild.md](company-skild.md)
- **Solution:** one foundation model for arbitrary embodiments (quadrupeds, humanoids, arms, mobile manipulators) with no prior body knowledge. **Delivery is explicitly SaaS/cloud-API:** manufacturers upload robot specs to **Skild Cloud**, a compiler auto-generates the control interface, users call high-level API endpoints or issue NL instructions. Trained at massive scale in NVIDIA Isaac Lab (sim) + Cosmos (synthetic data). Deploys on $4k–15k noisy hardware. Partners: ABB, Universal Robots, MiR, NVIDIA, Foxconn; acquired Zebra's robotics arm; **$1.4B Series C @ $14B+ (SoftBank, Jan 2026)**.
- **Is it really on-device? No — cloud-inference SaaS by design.** This is the *architectural inverse* of the on-device brief: the brain lives in Skild Cloud and streams to robots via API. Any latency-critical S1/S0 control (§L) still has to be local, which the SaaS framing does not address.
- **Strengths:** the cross-embodiment "compile-a-spec → API" abstraction is genuinely novel and the capital/partner signals are top-tier; a plausible play to be the *cloud* brain vendor.
- **Critical read:** reliability/"more robust autonomous operation" claims are **pre-deployment narrative (marketing)** — 2026 is a "deployment narrative" year, not audited deployment. For an *on-device* brain evaluation specifically, Skild scores lowest: it is the counter-example that proves the cloud-vs-edge split.

#### Chinese humanoid (cost-down + open)

**AgiBot (智元, GO-1 → GO-2)** — [company-agibot.md](company-agibot.md)
- **Solution:** GO-1 (Genie Operator-1, 2025-03) — ViLLA (InternVL2.5-2B VLM + latent-action planner + diffusion action expert), **open weights on HF**; GO-2 (2026-04) unifies reasoning+action in one architecture. Trained on the **open AgiBot World** dataset (>1M trajectories, 30 Hz collection).
- **Is it really on-device?** **Unclear — no board Hz published.** GO-1's cited **30 Hz is the AgiBot-World collection/eval rate, not proven onboard inference** (§I explicitly flags this as **(unverified)** — training rate ≠ real-machine perf). No dedicated edge SoC or onboard latency disclosed.
- **Strengths:** best open *data + model + sim + OS* story in China (the "Android play") — GO-1 open weights + >1M open trajectories are real assets outsiders can use/verify.
- **Critical read:** the on-device number is a **collection rate masquerading as performance** — exactly the §K trap ("训练率冒充板载率"). The June 23–28 2026 Longcheer (Nanchang) factory livestream — "64,828 tasks @99.99%" over ~64 h — was run on the **G2 wheeled mobile-manipulator hardware, not GO-1** (per AgiBot/press, verified), so it is *not* evidence for GO-1's onboard 30 Hz specifically; and Longcheer is AgiBot's own manufacturing partner and customer (AgiBot's 15,000th unit was delivered to Longcheer), i.e. a **company-run livestream with a commercially-tied partner (company-reported)** — better than a lab demo, not a third-party audit.

**UBTech (优必选, Walker S2 / Co-Agent / BrainNet)** — [company-ubtech.md](company-ubtech.md)
- **Solution (as of 2026):** **explicit split-brain — Intel Core i7 for real-time motion/balance + NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin for vision/navigation/AI inference in parallel.** BrainNet 2.0 = multi-robot shared-environment coordination; **Co-Agent = an edge agent running an LLM + smaller skill models locally, no remote-server round-trip** (multilingual NLP/planning/anomaly-detection on-board). Walker S2 in mass production, orders >¥800M; deployed at BYD, Geely, Foxconn, SF Express, Airbus (Jan 2026 border-customs pilot).
- **Is it really on-device?** **Yes for the Co-Agent/NLP + Orin AI tier** — UBTech explicitly states language/task decisions run on the robot, not the cloud. This is arguably the **most literal shipping instance of the §L 3-tier split**: i7 ≈ S0/S1 deterministic loop, Jetson Orin ≈ S1/S2 AI, Co-Agent ≈ S2 deliberation, all local.
- **Strengths:** concrete, named split-brain silicon (not "embedded GPUs, unspecified"); real factory customers + battery-swap for 24/7 duty; the clearest hardware realization of the reference architecture among all players.
- **Critical read:** no autonomy %, no MTBF/intervention-rate, no per-task-class success published — orders (¥800M) and factory presence are **commercial signals, not audited uptime (company-reported)**. Split-brain is well-executed; the *reliability* axis (§K Anchor 3) remains a void, as with everyone.

**Unitree (宇树, G1 + UnifoLM-VLA-0)** — [company-unitree.md](company-unitree.md)
- **Solution (2026-03):** **UnifoLM-VLA-0 open-sourced** (HF/GitHub) — built on **Qwen2.5-VL-7B**, a single policy network across 12 manipulation task classes (drawers, connectors, pick-and-place). Runs on the **G1 EDU's onboard Jetson Orin** (full Python/C++/ROS2 SDK). Trained on Unitree's **WBT teleoperation dataset** (claimed largest open humanoid teleop set).
- **Is it really on-device?** **Yes — open weights on onboard Jetson Orin is a real, reproducible on-device path** (§I roster). But Unitree itself states the **stock G1 "does not autonomously" walk novel spaces or complete novel multi-step tasks out of the box** — autonomy comes from the teleop-data→policy pipeline, not shipped behavior.
- **Strengths:** open-weight + onboard-Jetson + cheap hardware ($16k base G1, $4.9k R1) = the most *accessible* verifiable on-device brain; anyone can download UnifoLM-VLA-0 and reproduce.
- **Critical read:** same teleop-lineage caveat as Figure but *honestly disclosed* — Unitree doesn't over-claim autonomy. The gap is capability, not honesty: 12 task classes on a 7B model onboard Orin is a demo baseline, not a general worker **(company-reported for task coverage)**.

#### Module / chip suppliers — the platform layer

**Qualcomm (Dragonwing IQ10)** — [odm-competitors.md](odm-competitors.md)
- **Solution:** ~700 TOPS sparse (18 Oryon CPU + NPU + GPU), 64 GB LPDDR5x, **on-die Safety Island (SAIL, 2.5G Base-T safety domain)** + EtherCAT (4×1G) + 8×CAN-FD + up to 12 GMSL2 cameras + Wi-Fi 7; early-access June 2026, GA Sept 2026.
- **Critical read:** the **best-integrated realization of the §L stack in one package** — S0 Safety Island + fieldbus baked in, not a bolt-on FPGA. But it is an *enabler*, not an operator, and **field-unproven as a robot brain** as of 2026-07 (early-access only). Evaluate one deployment at a time, like GR00T.

**Quectel (移远, SH602HA-AP)** — [odm-competitors.md](odm-competitors.md), and the pitch target in [odm-opportunity]
- **Solution (CES 2026):** **D-Robotics Sunrise5 (X5M), ≤10 TOPS BPU** (octa-core Cortex-A55 @1.5 GHz, per Quectel) + Ubuntu 22.04, 40.5×40.5×2.9 mm, −25→+85 °C; supports Transformer/BEV/Occupancy, VSLAM, binocular depth, 3D point cloud, LiDAR fusion; **pairs with Quectel's own LTE Cat1/Cat4/5G/Wi-Fi6/GNSS modules** for connectivity. (RAM/flash config **(unverified)** — Quectel's own product page lists dimensions/temp but no memory; secondary sources disagree, e.g. "4 GB + 32 GB" vs "4 GB + 64 GB".)
- **Critical read:** a **real, shipping "arms-dealer" module** — compute die + connectivity + OS pre-integrated, exactly the §L module-maker wedge (integration, not TOPS). **But 10 TOPS ≠ Thor (2,070 TFLOPS) / IQ10 (700 TOPS)** — it sits at the **perception / S1-lite tier**, not a full-VLA S2 brain. It proves the *play* exists today; it does not yet host a frontier on-device brain.

### 3. Patterns / 读数 (synthesis)

**A. Genuinely on-device (inference-local) vs cloud/teleop-dependent — the real split:**
- **Genuinely inference-local (verifiable):** Figure Helix, DeepMind Gemini-On-Device (design), UBTech Co-Agent, Unitree UnifoLM-VLA on Jetson Orin, and *any* GR00T/π0 deployment quantized onto Jetson Thor. These run the brain on the robot's own silicon.
- **Cloud- or server-dependent by default:** **π0 (PI's own websocket-server default), Skild (cloud-API SaaS)** — the two clearest "not on-device out of the box" cases, both self-documented.
- **Teleop-dependent for actual autonomy:** **Tesla Optimus** (confirmed teleop in flagship demos) is the extreme; but the deeper pattern is that **every VLA here is teleop-trained** (Figure ~500 h, Unitree WBT, AgiBot World, Gemini's ALOHA demos) — so "autonomous" universally means "autonomous imitation of teleop trajectories." The honest distinction is **inference-local (many) vs autonomy-discovered-not-imitated (essentially none, publicly).**

**B. Open vs closed:**
- **Open weights:** GR00T (N1.6, HF), openpi (π0/π0.5), AgiBot GO-1, Unitree UnifoLM-VLA-0, plus edge-natives SmolVLA/TinyVLA/BitVLA (§I). Open ≈ *evaluable* — outsiders can verify latency/quality.
- **Closed:** Figure Helix (S1 weights), Gemini Robotics, Tesla Optimus, Skild Brain. Closed ≈ *unverifiable* — you get the marketing number and nothing to reproduce it. **The correlation is stark: the closed players (Tesla, Figure, Skild) are exactly the ones whose autonomy claims are least independently supported.**

**C. Platform vs end-solution:**
- **Platforms (win regardless of any single robot's autonomy):** NVIDIA (GR00T+Thor+Isaac), Qualcomm (IQ10), and at the module layer Quectel/D-Robotics. Grade these as infrastructure — strong where adopted, but with **no autonomy claim to hold accountable**.
- **End-solutions (own an autonomy claim):** Figure, Tesla, Gemini, AgiBot, UBTech, Unitree, Skild. These are the ones the §K rubric bites on — and where MTBF/third-party-eval voids matter.

**D. Western-frontier vs Chinese cost-down split:**
- **Western frontier** competes on *model capability + narrative* (biggest VLM, longest livestream, custom silicon) and is mostly **closed** (Figure/Tesla/Gemini) with two open exceptions (NVIDIA, PI). Marketing intensity is highest; independent evidence is weakest at the top (Tesla, Figure).
- **Chinese humanoid** competes on *cost + deployment volume + openness* (AgiBot/Unitree open weights, UBTech's ¥800M orders, $4.9k–16k hardware) and ships **concrete, named split-brain silicon** (UBTech i7+Orin) rather than "unspecified embedded GPUs." The Chinese camp is **more honest about teleop lineage** (Unitree explicitly disclaims stock autonomy) but leans on **company-run factory livestreams** (AgiBot Longcheer, UBTech) instead of third-party audits — same evidence gap, different framing.

**E. Honest verdict on the on-device-brain state of the art (as of 2026-07):**
1. **Inference-local is solved-enough; autonomy is not.** Running a dual-system VLA onboard at S1 = 200 Hz (Figure) / S2 = 7–11 Hz (GR00T/π0.5 on Thor) is a **real, shipped capability** — but only by shrinking S1 (80M) and exiling reasoning to a slow S2 tier. Full VLAs still crawl at 3–19 Hz on the best edge silicon (§K/§L).
2. **The evaluation void is the story.** Not one player publishes MTBF/intervention-rate at 8h×5d duty (§K Anchor 3); not one shows a third-party pairwise (RoboArena-style) result; almost none give per-task-class OOD success on the *shipping* SoC. Every headline is a SKU-less, duty-cycle-less number — reject it per the §K rule.
3. **The most-marketed are the least-verified.** Tesla and Figure generate the most autonomy headlines and have the weakest independent evidence (Tesla: confirmed teleop; Figure: uncorroborated livestreams). The most *trustworthy* are the open, reproducible ones (PI, Unitree, GR00T) — precisely because you can download and check them.

**F. The module-maker (Quectel) read — where the gap is:**
- **The frontier players leave a specific hole:** the compute/model layer (NVIDIA, PI, DeepMind) and the robot-brain layer (Figure, UBTech, Unitree) both assume *someone else* delivers the **pre-integrated, connectivity-enabled, functionally-safe module** that stitches S2+S1 (big SoC) to S0 (deterministic MCU/Safety-Island) to fieldbus (EtherCAT/CAN-FD) to cellular (5G/Wi-Fi for the S2 cloud-assist tier) with the non-portable per-vendor runtime already ported (§L module-maker read). **That integration — not the TOPS number — is the scarce good.**
- **Quectel's SH602HA-AP proves the wedge exists but sits too low:** at ≤10 TOPS it's a *perception/S1-lite* module, not a full-VLA S2 brain. The gap a brain-module could fill is a **Thor/IQ10-class compute die + Quectel's cellular stack + a deterministic S0 island + a ported runtime**, sold as one FuSa-certified module — the "arms-dealer" play (see [ODM competitors](odm-competitors.md), [ODM opportunity](odm-opportunity.md)). No one currently ships that at the flagship tier: NVIDIA sells the die but not the cellular/FuSa integration; Qualcomm's IQ10 bundles S0+fieldbus but is field-unproven and not a module-maker's SKU; the robot OEMs each re-integrate it in-house. **A cellular-module vendor that ships the flagship-compute + connectivity + S0 + porting-layer as one module sells exactly the thing the frontier leaves unbuilt.**
