> The physical hardware that manufactures robot training data splits into three families: (A) **embodiment-free / robot-free rigs** (UMI-class handheld grippers, egocentric cameras, instrumented gloves) that let a human generate demonstrations with no robot present; (B) **teleoperation rigs** (ALOHA-class leader-follower arms, VR controllers, haptic/mocap exoskeletons) that puppet a real robot; and (C) **on-robot** capture (a robot doing real work under mixed autonomy/teleop). The core tradeoff is fixed by physics: robot-free rigs are 5–20× cheaper and 3× faster per demo but carry an **embodiment gap** (a handheld gripper is not an arm) and **recovered — not measured — action labels** (SLAM/VIO estimates the end-effector pose, so mm-to-cm error and drift enter the training data); teleoperation gives exact, sensor-measured robot actions but is expensive, slow, and capped by operator fatigue (~35 demos/h, hard human ceiling). This page goes deep on the **device hardware** — BOMs, sensors, tracking method, throughput, and a critical read of each — then two deeper sections: a per-system **engineering-design breakdown** (每个装置的三模块架构:感知装置 → 位姿/动作恢复 → 数据格式) and the **China data-collection ecosystem** (state 数据训练场 + hardware-vendor cost-down + the "genrobot" resolution). For the data *economics* (price/hour, foundry business) see [Data foundry](data-foundry.md); for the *dataset* survey and scaling laws see [Data](data.md); for the imitation-learning lineage see [Dexterous manipulation](manipulation.md).

## 1. Taxonomy + tradeoff table

Three families, one axis: **how directly is the collected action label tied to the deployment robot?**

| | (A) Embodiment-free / robot-free | (B) Teleoperation rig | (C) On-robot |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | Human holds a gripper / wears a camera or glove; no robot present | Human puppets a real robot via leader arms, VR, or exo | Robot does real work under mixed teleop/autonomy |
| **Exemplar devices** | UMI, FastUMI, DexUMI, Dobb·E Stick, DexCap, Generalist "data hands", Aria/Vision Pro egocentric | ALOHA 2, Cobot Magic, ARX R5, DROID (Quest 2), AgiBot A2 VR/mocap, ActiveUMI | Apptronik Robot Park, 1X Expert Mode, deployed cobot fleets |
| **Rig cost** | $25 (Dobb·E) – ~$400 (UMI) – ~$3.5k (DexCap glove) | ~$22k stationary ALOHA 2 4-arm (~$17.4k arms); ~$32k only with mobile base + robot; VR ~$300–3.5k + robot | Robot cost ($10k–$100k+); no extra rig |
| **Throughput (per operator)** | ~111 demos/h (UMI); ~231/h bare hand; passive/continuous for egocentric | ~35 demos/h (teleop ceiling); ALOHA fleets 100s–1,000s demos/day across many rigs | Bounded by real task pace; runs unattended |
| **Embodiment gap** | **Large** — handheld gripper/hand ≠ target arm dynamics, reach, mass; base motion ≠ wheeled base | **Zero-to-small** — data is on (or morphologically matched to) the deployment robot | **Zero** — data *is* the deployment robot |
| **Action-label fidelity** | **Recovered** post-hoc via VI-SLAM/VIO/ARKit — 6mm (UMI SLAM) to 24mm (T265) position error, can drift/fail | **Measured** — exact joint encoders / motor commands at 15–50 Hz | **Measured** — exact robot proprioception |
| **Force/tactile** | Usually none (vision+geometry only); exceptions: DexUMI, Genesis/Generalist gloves add tactile | Optional (F/T sensors on ALOHA 2, AgiBot 6-axis); most rigs vision+proprioception | Whatever the robot has |
| **Scalability** | **Highest** — crowd-source "in the wild" by non-experts, no lab/robot/MoCap | **Low–medium** — operator-bound, rig-bound, site-bound | Medium — limited by deployed-robot count |
| **Wins on** | Cost, scale, environment diversity, dynamic/bimanual pick-place | Clean labels for contact-rich, force-sensitive, last-mile reliability | Zero-gap data + real-work revenue offset |
| **Loses on** | Force tasks, precise insertion, exact target kinematics, QA (drift/mislabel) | $, speed, operator fatigue, embodiment lock-in | Fleet size, safety, cost per hour |

**Which family wins on what (as of 2026-07):**
- **Scale + diversity + $**: family A. The entire 2025–2026 wearables pivot (Generalist 500k+ h, Figure Go-Big, NVIDIA EgoScale) is a bet that cheap human data at volume beats expensive robot data — backed by scaling-law evidence (see [Data](data.md)).
- **Clean labels for the last mile**: family B. Contact-rich, force-critical, and precision-insertion tasks still need exact action labels; teleop is where reliability comes from (π*0.6/RECAP fine-tunes on teleop corrections — see [Manipulation](manipulation.md)).
- **Zero gap + revenue**: family C. Deployed-fleet data (Apptronik, 1X) closes the loop but is throughput-limited by how many robots are actually working.
- The 2026 consensus is **hybrid**: pretrain on cheap family-A human data, fine-tune the last mile on family-B/C robot data. No lab bets on one family alone.

## 系统方案设计详解 (Detailed System Designs)

> Every data-collection rig in this survey — no matter how different the hardware looks — reduces to the **same three-block architecture**: (1) a **sensing rig** (a camera + IMU, an instrumented linkage, or a joint-encoder chain rigidly fixed to the point that touches the world), (2) a **pose/action-recovery pipeline** that turns raw sensor streams into a supervised action label (either *recovered* post-hoc by SLAM/VIO/ARKit, or *measured* directly off encoders/motor commands), and (3) a **data format** that packs synchronized observation + action + gripper/hand state into a replay buffer (Zarr, HDF5, RLDS, or LeRobot). The entire design space is the set of engineering choices inside those three blocks, and every choice is a bet on one of two axes from the [§1 tradeoff matrix](#1-taxonomy--tradeoff-table): **label fidelity** (recovered mm-error vs. measured-exact) and **embodiment gap** (is the sensing rig kinematically the deployment end-effector, or a human proxy?). The blocks below give the per-system engineering breakdown the survey rows abbreviate. See also [Data](data.md) for the resulting datasets, [Dexterous manipulation](manipulation.md) for the policies trained on them, and [Tactile & hands](tactile-hands.md) for the force-sensing subsystems most of these rigs lack.

### UMI (Universal Manipulation Interface, 通用操作接口)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: 3D-printed (PLA) parallel-jaw gripper body (2-finger, trigger-free, spring/pulley close) + **GoPro Hero9 Black** as the sole sensor/recorder + **GoPro Max Lens Mod** (155° fisheye) screwed on + **two rear-facing acrylic/mirror strips** in the camera's peripheral FoV for "implicit stereo" side-view + a **fiducial (checkerboard/dot) marker** printed inside the jaws to make finger-width visible to the same feed + soft/compliant 3D-printed fingers (TPU-tipped, series-elastic principle) + GoPro's **built-in 6-axis IMU** logged to the .mp4 GPMF track (no separate IMU chip) + **GoPro Labs firmware** for QR-triggered hardware start/stop sync + GoPro's internal battery. *(primary)* Actuated fork replaces manual spring-close with a **DYNAMIXEL XL430-W250-T** servo + **U2D2** interface/power hub + **MGN7C 80mm** linear rails + 5mm flat belt + constant-force springs (0.1×8×110). *(secondary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: GoPro Hero9 + Max Lens Mod, 155° diagonal FoV, up to 1080p/2.7K in MAX SuperView — this **single fisheye feed does double duty**: policy RGB observation *and* VI-SLAM pose recovery. IMU: GoPro internal 6-axis (~200Hz gyro/accel) **muxed into the GPMF metadata track of the same MP4** — the "hardware sync" trick: video + IMU + audio share one hardware clock → **zero cross-sensor latency to calibrate**. Mirrors: 2× planar mirrors bracket-mounted ~45° reflect the gripper side into the same frame → stereo-like depth from one monocular camera. Fiducial: printed dot pattern inside the jaw, regressed offline to continuous jaw width (no encoder in base design). *(primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: 2-DoF functional unit — jaw open/close is the only actuated DoF; the "6-DoF" comes from **free-hand wrist motion recovered by SLAM, not mechanical sensing**. Weight ~780g; dims ~310×175×210mm; **finger stroke 80mm chosen to match WSG-50/Robotiq grippers** so trajectories replay directly on a UR5e/Franka fitted with identical gripper geometry (this is the whole embodiment-gap-closing trick). GoPro on a rigid printed cage → camera-to-tip transform fixed, calibrated once. *(primary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** — human hand-holds the gripper, GoPro records fisheye RGB + IMU + jaw-fiducial as one MP4/demo; GoPro Labs QR-flash hardware-syncs multiple units. **Processing** — batch offline SLAM in Docker (`chicheng/orb_slam3`, Steffen Urban's GoPro-tuned **ORB-SLAM3** fork) ingests fisheye+IMU in monocular-inertial mode → per-frame 6-DoF gripper pose; intrinsics/IMU-cam extrinsics pre-calibrated once via **OpenImuCameraCalibrator**. **Action-label recovery** — SLAM poses = ground-truth EE trajectory; jaw width regressed separately from the fiducial crop (small ConvNet, Dobb·E-style). **Reported tracking error: ~6.1mm / 3.5° per-gripper absolute ATE, ~10.1mm / 0.8° inter-gripper RPE (relative pose error between the two grippers, not inter-frame)** *(primary, paper-reported, Fig. 12)*. **Latency-matching**: a filmed QR/flash code measures GoPro-to-GoPro and camera-to-robot latency empirically → actions shifted/interpolated at inference so runtime obs-action lag matches training. **Format**: **SE(3) relative** (not absolute) pose actions + continuous gripper-width channel, packed into a **Zarr replay buffer**; frames kept as the **original undistorted fisheye** (policy sees fisheye directly, no rectification). Policy: Diffusion Policy w/ CLIP-pretrained ViT-L/14. *(primary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: ORB-SLAM3 (Steffen-Urban fork, Dockerized); OpenImuCameraCalibrator; `scripts_slam_pipeline/` in real-stanford/universal_manipulation_interface (~99.9% Python, Conda) → Zarr → Diffusion Policy (PyTorch). Robot side: 3Dconnexion SpaceMouse + UR5/UR5e or Franka driver. Actuated variant: Dynamixel SDK over U2D2. *(primary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: gripper (printed body + soft fingers + screws + mirrors + fiducial) **~$73**; GoPro Hero9 + Max Lens Mod + mounts **~$298**; **total ~$371** (paper figures) *(primary)*. Actuated fork adds XL430 servo (~$50–70) + U2D2 (~$40–50) + 2× MGN7C rails + belt + springs — community "low hundreds added", no published total *(unverified)*.
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: GoPro chosen over a custom cam+IMU board because it's off-the-shelf, ruggedized, and — critically — **bundles a hardware-synced IMU with video, killing a whole class of timestamp-fusion bugs cheaply**. 155° fisheye trades center resolution for peripheral context: keeps fingertips + object in frame under large wrist rotation *and* feeds ORB-SLAM3's wide-baseline tracking. Mirrors = cheap implicit stereo with no second camera/cable/sync. Soft fingers absorb hand jitter and generalize contact. **Relative SE(3) actions** so the same policy works on fixed or mobile base and small SLAM drift doesn't compound. The authors' own stated **most-fragile part: ORB-SLAM3 tracking loss/drift in textureless / fast-motion scenes**. *(primary)*

### FastUMI (Fast Universal Manipulation Interface)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: Two-part design — a **handheld collect unit** and a **robot-mounted infer unit** — deliberately **hardware-decoupled** across ~5 grippers (xArm, Robotiq 2F-85 …) vs. UMI's WSG-50 lock-in. Handheld = **Intel RealSense T265** tracking module + **GoPro Hero9** fisheye + fingertip fiducials for aperture + **Elgato HD60 X** capture card. Dual-arm variant = 2× T265 + 2× GoPro. *(primary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **RealSense T265** — a self-contained VIO module (dual fisheye + built-in IMU + on-chip pose) outputting **direct 6-DoF pose, no external SLAM**. GoPro Hero9 fisheye at **1280×720 @60fps** for policy RGB. Fingertip fiducials → gripper aperture. *(primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: UMI-class parallel-jaw handheld, but **drops UMI's side mirrors** to free gripper space (loses implicit-stereo depth). Because it's gripper-agnostic, the mechanical body is re-printed per target gripper rather than locked to one. *(primary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** via ROS @20Hz. **Action-label recovery**: T265 emits pose directly → **zero SLAM calibration**, but **task-dependent ~10.5mm (best, "pick cup") to ~24.1mm (worst, "rearrange coke") position error** — coarser than UMI's 6.1mm (the oft-cited "~2.37cm" sits near the high end). *(primary)* FastUMI-100K corpus (arXiv 2510.08022): **>100k trajectories, 54 tasks, 120–500 frames/traj**; commonly-quoted "~600h / ~3 months" is *(unverified — not in the abstract)*.
- **软件栈 (software)**: ROS-based capture; Elgato capture card for the GoPro HDMI stream; off-the-shelf T265 SDK for pose. *(primary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: not disclosed; off-the-shelf T265 + GoPro ≈ "several hundred $/unit", claimed cheaper in aggregate than SLAM/MoCap builds *(company claim)*.
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: A direct **throughput-vs-fidelity trade** — cm-level zero-calibration tracking swaps out mm-level SLAM. Matters for precision insertion, but eliminates UMI's per-device IMU-cam calibration burden entirely. Note: **Intel discontinued the T265**, a real supply risk for any T265-based rig going forward. *(primary)*

### DexUMI (dexterous exoskeleton hand interface)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: A **wearable exoskeleton glove** — a rigid 3D-printed link-and-joint mechanism strapped over the human fingers — instrumented with **one rotary encoder per actuated joint** measuring finger flexion/extension directly on the exo linkage; plus a **wrist-mounted camera** placed where the target robot hand's wrist camera will sit. Validated on two robot hands: underactuated **6-DoF Inspire Hand** and fully-actuated **12-DoF XHand**. *(secondary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **Rotary encoders (Alps RDC506018A-class** cited in the sensing lineage) — one per actuated joint, reading human joint angles **mechanically, not via vision** → immune to the self-occlusion that breaks vision-only hand-pose estimators (HaMeR-style). Single wrist camera (model/res not disclosed) → training RGB after inpainting. **No tactile sensor in base DexUMI** (tactile variants are follow-on work). *(secondary; unverified encoder count)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: Rigid printed exo, DoF matched to the target robot hand (up to 12 for XHand, 6 for Inspire) so each encoder maps ~1:1 to a robot finger-joint command. Worn like a brace (not gripped like UMI); the operator's own wrist supplies the 6-DoF wrist motion (tracked like a UMI wrist cam). *(secondary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** — operator wears the exo and works at natural speed with direct haptic/proprioceptive feedback; encoders stream joint angles synchronized to wrist video. **Action-label recovery** — a **regression model** (calibrated per-joint from paired canonical poses) maps encoder values → robot motor commands, compensating for backlash/friction nonlinearity; paper notes **precision is typically guaranteed in only one direction (open OR close) per joint** — a disclosed hardware limitation. **Visual-gap closure** — recorded video is segmented with **SAM2**, the hand+exo pixels erased, and the region filled by **ProPainter** flow-based video inpainting (optionally compositing the robot hand) so training frames resemble the deployed robot's own camera view. **Result: 86% avg task success across the two hands; claimed 3.2× data-collection efficiency vs. teleop.** *(secondary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: **SAM2** (Ravi et al. 2024) segmentation + **ProPainter** (Zhou et al. 2023) inpainting + a per-joint regression model (architecture not disclosed) → downstream imitation-learning policy. real-stanford/DexUMI repo. *(secondary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **not disclosed** in accessible sources (no published BOM table); likely low-hundreds-to-low-thousands DIY by UMI norms *(unverified)*.
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: Solves the **kinematic gap** between human and robot hand by sensing joints mechanically (occlusion-immune) and enabling natural-speed haptic demos → the 3.2× efficiency claim. The trade this *creates* is a **visual gap** (training frames show a chunky exo, not the robot hand), answered **entirely in software** (SAM2+ProPainter) to keep the wearable cheap. The need for **both** a mechanical remap *and* a visual inpaint is an implicit admission the naive approach fails; inpainting injects its own fidelity risk (synthetic hand ≠ ground truth). *(secondary)*

### Dobb·E ("The Stick" + iPhone)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: **"The Stick"** = a **$25 off-the-shelf Vive suction-cup reacher-grabber** as the mechanical backbone; stock padded tips swapped for **3D-printed cylindrical tips** (better on thin handles); a 3D-printed iPhone mount (top/bottom/knob/hex-nut border, M5 socket screws) clamped near the pivot; an **iPhone 12 Pro or newer (LiDAR required)** running the **Record3D** app as the entire sensing/recording package — **no separate camera, IMU chip, or SLAM computer on the tool**. For deployment, the same mount bolts an iPhone to a **Hello Robot Stretch RE1** wrist; an onboard **Intel NUC** runs the policy, streaming RGB-D over USB. *(primary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: iPhone 12 Pro+ built-in stack — rear **RGB @1280×720**, rear **LiDAR depth @256×192**, internal IMU/gyro fused by Apple's on-board **ARKit-class VIO** → **6D pose (trans+rot) @30fps**, all via Record3D's proprietary `.r3d` format. **No external SLAM, no markers.** Gripper open/close is **NOT physically sensed** — recovered purely visually in post. *(primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: spring-loaded trigger squeeze → 1-DoF pincer; rigid iPhone mount fixes phone-to-jaw geometry so the phone's 6D pose stands in for gripper pose. Same mount geometry reused on the Stretch RE1 wrist → sensor viewpoint consistent between human demo and robot execution. *(primary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** — operator walks a home holding the Stick recording first-person RGB-D + 6D pose @30fps into `.r3d`; ~10 min/home over a 5×5/4×6 start grid → **"Homes of New York" (HoNY): 22 homes, 5,620 demos, 13h, ~1.5M frames**. **Action-label recovery** — 6D pose from the iPhone's hardware VIO, **explicitly chosen over classical SfM (OpenSfM)** because "SfM fails as soon as the camera gets very close to any surface and loses all visual features" (a tested-and-rejected alternative). **Gripper-state recovery** — a **3-layer ConvNet** trained on **500 hand-labeled frames** regresses continuous [0,1] tip-distance from RGB → **0.035 MSE** on held-out val (the learned analog to UMI's fiducial). **Format** — imgs 256×256; actions stored as **absolute 6D iPhone pose @30Hz**, converted to relative deltas at train/runtime; final **7D action** (3 trans + 3 axis-angle + 1 gripper). **Representation** — ResNet34 self-supervised with **MoCo-v3, 60 epochs** on HoNY → "Home Pretrained Representations" (HPR), beating R3M/VC-1/MVP/ImageNet by ≥23%. **Policy** — 2 FC layers on the 512-d HPR embedding → 7D action, per-axis-normalized MSE, lr 3e-5, 50 epochs, subsampled 3.75Hz. **Deploy** — NUC @30Hz → **PyKDL IK solver that adds the camera frame as a joint directly in the URDF** (no explicit camera-to-EE calibration at runtime). **81% success across 109 tasks in 10 novel homes.** *(primary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: Record3D (commercial iOS app) for capture; custom Python `.r3d`→tensor pipeline; MoCo-v3 pretraining (PyTorch); **PyKDL** IK. *(primary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **~$25 stick** + user's own iPhone — the cheapest rig in this survey. *(primary)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: ARKit-class on-device VIO removes UMI's entire SLAM-tuning burden but **ties fidelity to Apple's black-box tracker (no published ATE)**. A reacher-grabber's kinematics differ from a robot arm even more than UMI's WSG-50-matched jaw → **larger embodiment gap**. Wins on dead-simple in-home scale; loses on precision/force/exact-kinematics tasks. *(primary)*

### DexCap (mocap-glove chest rig)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: Portable body-worn rig — **Rokoko EMF gloves** (finger joints) + one SLAM camera per glove (6-DoF wrist pose) + a **RealSense L515 LiDAR** on a 3D-printed chest harness + **3× RealSense T265** below + **Intel NUC + power bank in a backpack**; runs @**60Hz**. *(secondary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **Rokoko EMF (electromagnetic-field) gloves** for finger joint angles — **occlusion-robust** at the moment of grasp, a real edge over vision-only hand tracking. Per-glove SLAM camera for wrist 6-DoF; **L515 solid-state LiDAR** for chest-frame 3D scene context; **3× T265** for VIO. *(secondary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: no gripper — the human hand is the end-effector; the chest harness (3D-printed) fixes L515/T265 geometry. The EMF glove supplies dexterous finger DoF that a UMI parallel jaw cannot. *(secondary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** — "in the wild" daily-activity capture, **~40 min/session** (backpack battery cap). **Action-label recovery** — per-glove SLAM cam gives wrist SE(3); EMF gloves give finger joints directly; L515+T265 place the hands in a scene frame. **Training** — the **DexIL** imitation algorithm learns dexterous skills from the fused mocap+RGBD. *(secondary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: Rokoko SDK (EMF); RealSense SDK (L515/T265); DexIL training stack. *(secondary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **~$3.1k–$3.5k** hardware (Rokoko-based build; exact figure not in paper) *(unverified)*.
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: EMF finger tracking is **occlusion-robust** where vision fails (grasp contact) — but glove mocap ≠ robot-hand joint limits (the **same dexterous embodiment gap as DexUMI, without the exo's mechanical remap**). Chest LiDAR gives 3D scene context most handhelds lack; the 40-min battery caps session length. *(secondary)*

### ALOHA 2 (teleoperation leader-follower workcell)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: Bimanual parallel-jaw workcell — **2× ViperX 6-DoF followers + 2× WidowX leaders** (Trossen Robotics), kinematically matched; a **low-friction rail** for ergonomic leading; **4× RealSense D405** cameras (2× wrist + overhead + worm's-eye); a **MuJoCo sim twin**. *(primary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **4× RealSense D405** short-range RGB-D. Action state comes from the **leader-arm joint encoders** (Dynamixel-based ViperX/WidowX) — **measured, not recovered**. *(primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: leader (WidowX) and follower (ViperX) share kinematics → **direct joint-space mapping, no IK**; the human back-drives the low-friction leader and the follower mirrors it. **14-DoF** total (2×7). *(primary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture** — human back-drives leaders; **follower joint positions logged @50Hz + gripper aperture** = the exact action label (**zero-to-small embodiment gap**, gold-standard fidelity). **Format** — **14-DoF joint positions @50Hz** stored as **HDF5** episodes (the ALOHA/ACT convention), directly portable to **LeRobot**. *(primary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: ALOHA/ACT open-source stack; MuJoCo twin for sim eval; HDF5 → LeRobot for community training. *(primary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **~$17.4k arms** (2× ViperX ≈ $11k + 2× WidowX ≈ $6.4k) → **~$22k** complete stationary 4-arm build (cameras/frame/PC). The **~$32k** figure adds a **Mobile-ALOHA base** (~$10k AgileX Tracer) and is **not** a stationary price. The paper itself gives **no dollar figure** (cost is third-party BOM). *(primary hardware; secondary cost)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: **Zero-to-small embodiment gap + exact measured joint actions** = the gold standard for label fidelity. The wall is **cost (~$22k/station) + one human per station + the ~35 demos/h fatigue ceiling** — operator-bound, not hardware-bound. The de-facto academic standard and the template for Chinese data-center rigs. See [Manipulation](manipulation.md). *(primary)*

### GELLO (kinematically-matched leader arm)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: A **low-cost leader arm built as a kinematic replica** of the target follower, so each leader joint maps 1:1 to a follower joint (no IK, intuitive joint-space teleop). Structure = **3D-printed links** custom-matched to the target robot + a chain of **DYNAMIXEL servos used purely as high-resolution absolute encoders** (passively back-driven by the operator). *(secondary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **DYNAMIXEL XL330-series** servos as the joint sensors — **12-bit encoders, ~0.088° mechanical resolution** — read as the action label; robot's own cameras supply observations. *(secondary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: printed links reproduce the follower's link lengths/joint axes so the leader *is* a scale kinematic twin; the operator hand-guides the leader, follower mirrors joint angles. *(secondary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Action-label recovery = measured** — leader encoder angles ARE the follower command; no SLAM/VIO, no post-hoc estimation. **Format** — joint-space trajectories, HDF5 per robot; force-augmented forks (**FACTR**, "GELLO+Force") add gripper F/T for contact-rich learning. *(secondary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: Dynamixel SDK; open GELLO codebase; per-robot URDF for the printed twin. *(secondary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **~$270 (original single-arm BOM)** to **~$363 per leader arm** in later builds — dominated by the Dynamixel servos. *(secondary)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: The cheapest path to **exact, measured, joint-space labels** — trading UMI's robot-free economics for a hard kinematic match (needs a printed twin per target robot, and a follower robot present at collection). Wins on label fidelity + zero-IK intuitiveness; loses on portability (one twin per arm) and on force (base design is vision+proprioception only, hence the FACTR force forks). *(secondary)*

### DROID (Franka + Quest 2 VR rig)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: A **standardized single-rig** design replicated across 13 institutions — **Franka Panda 7-DoF** arm + **Meta Quest 2** controllers for 6-DoF EE + gripper teleop + **2× ZED 2 stereo + 1× ZED Mini** (wrist) cameras on a **portable height-adjustable desk**. *(primary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **ZED 2 / ZED Mini** stereo (full-HD, 3 views + depth); **Franka joint encoders** for proprioception; Quest 2 controllers for the 6-DoF teleop target. *(primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: Franka 7-DoF follower; VR gives **EE-space (not joint) teleop** — the operator moves a Quest controller, IK drives the arm. Standardized desk → high scene diversity per calibrated setup. *(primary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Capture @15Hz**, camera data stored in **ZED `.svo`** (converted to `.mp4` for release). **Action-label recovery = measured** — each trajectory logs **7D joint pos+vel, 6D EE pose+vel (base frame), 1D gripper pos+vel**; the DROID base policy outputs **joint-velocity commands** (action space kept in **joint space** to avoid compounding errors from Cartesian conversion). **Format** — released in **RLDS** (TFDS-style, full-HD stereo + all low-dim) *and* **raw `trajectory.h5`** (HDF5 low-dim + `.svo` video). **76k trajectories / 350h / 564 scenes / 86 tasks over 12 months.** *(primary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: ZED SDK (`.svo`); DROID data pipeline (`droid_dataset_builder`) → RLDS/TFDS; Franka + Quest 2 drivers. *(primary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **Franka ~$25–30k + Quest 2 ~$300 + ZED cams** — robot-bound (the arm dominates cost). *(primary)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: One standardized calibrated rig → maximal scene diversity and cross-lab reproducibility; VR gives cheap 6-DoF teleop but **only EE-space control (no direct joint puppeting)**. Clean measured labels; **robot-bound and operator-bound**. RLDS makes it the reference in-the-wild manipulation dataset. See [Data](data.md). *(primary)*

### Open-TeleVision / VR immersive teleop (incl. iDP3, AgiBot VR)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: A VR headset (**Apple Vision Pro** or Quest) streams the operator's **hand + head + wrist poses in SE(3)** to a server that does human→robot retargeting; a **head-mounted stereo camera on the robot actively follows the operator's head** for immersive first-person feedback. Applied to humanoids (e.g. Fourier GR1 with Inspire Hands in iDP3; Unitree/G1). *(secondary)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: VR device on-board hand/head/wrist tracking (SE(3)); robot **active stereo head camera** for egocentric observation; iDP3 adds a head **RealSense L515 LiDAR** for 3D point-cloud policy input (which added ~0.5s teleop latency). *(secondary; iDP3 primary)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: teleoperates a **real deployment robot** (zero embodiment gap). Dexterous hands sync via **7 keypoint vectors**; grippers via a single thumb-index vector. *(secondary)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Action-label recovery = measured on-robot** — VR SE(3) targets → **dex-retargeting optimization (SLSQP)** → robot joint commands, logged with the robot's own proprioception. **Format** — robot-native HDF5 + retargeted joint trajectories, typically LeRobot-portable. Reported: up to **100% success on towel-fold / can-sort** (Open-TeleVision); iDP3 collected only **10 demos/task**. *(secondary)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: **dex-retargeting** library (SLSQP real-time optimization); OpenTeleVision codebase; robot-specific controllers. *(secondary)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: **Apple Vision Pro ~$3,500** (or Quest ~$300) **+ the deployment humanoid** (tens of $k). *(secondary; AVP price primary)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: **Zero embodiment gap** (data is on the deployment robot) and immersive active vision → high-precision dexterous teleop — but **near-zero scalability**: iDP3's authors state verbatim that AVP teleop is *"tiring for human teleoperators, making imitation data hard to scale up within the research lab."* This is the **exact opposite pole from UMI** and the clearest illustration of *why* the field pays for robot-free capture despite the gap. *(secondary; iDP3 fatigue quote primary)*

### Aria / egocentric glasses + data gloves / mocap suits (passive human capture)
- **系统组成 (composition)**: Passive human-worn sensors that record demonstrations with **no robot and no explicit action label** — head-worn glasses (**Meta Project Aria**, Apple Vision Pro egocentric/EgoDex, Tesla's 5-camera helmet+backpack), instrumented **data gloves** (Generalist "data hands", **Genesis tactile e-skin glove**), and full-body **mocap suits** (Neura's 1,000+ industrial workers). *(primary for Aria/EgoDex; company-reported for gloves/Tesla)*
- **传感器栈 (sensors)**: **Aria Gen 2** — 12MP RGB + **4 CV/SLAM cameras** (front stereo, HDR) + **2 IMUs** + magnetometer + barometer + GNSS + **eye-tracking (per-eye gaze, vergence, pupil, blink)** + PPG heart-rate + 7-mic array + contact mic; **on-device SLAM/eye/hand tracking on custom silicon**. EgoDex (Vision Pro): on-device hand/finger tracking → 829h + paired 3D hand joints, 194 tasks. Genesis glove: **tactile-sensing e-skin** claiming a **1:1:1 glove↔human-hand↔robot-hand mapping**. *(Aria/EgoDex primary; Genesis company-reported)*
- **机械/硬件 (mechanical)**: no gripper/arm — the human body is the effector; glasses are worn, gloves fit the hand, suits fit the body. Genesis claims "bone-for-bone" glove↔robot-hand geometry. *(mixed)*
- **数据管线 (pipeline)**: **Action-label recovery** — Aria/EgoDex yield **hand-pose only, no robot action labels, no force**; retargeting hand→gripper/robot-hand is the open bridge (EgoMimic co-training: **90 min Aria → up to 400% gain**). Gloves add finger joints (+ tactile for Genesis). Mocap suits add full-body joints. **Format** — Aria: **VRS** multi-stream container; gloves/suits: vendor mocap formats; all need a downstream retarget step to become robot actions. *(Aria/EgoMimic primary; glove claims company-reported)*
- **软件栈 (software)**: `projectaria_tools` (VRS, on-device SLAM/eye/hand); Rokoko/vendor mocap SDKs; Genesis/Generalist proprietary stacks. *(mixed)*
- **成本 (BOM)**: Aria = research device (not retail-priced); Apple Vision Pro ~$3,500; Generalist/Genesis gloves "cheap"/"100× less" *(company claim, unverified)*; Tesla/Neura not disclosed. *(mixed)*
- **关键设计取舍 (design read)**: Cheapest, most-scalable capture (passive, crowd-sourceable, environment-diverse) — the entire 2025–26 wearables pivot (**Generalist 500k+ h, Figure Go-Big, NVIDIA EgoScale**) bets cheap human data at volume beats expensive robot data. The structural weakness: **hand-pose-only, no measured robot action, and (except Genesis/DexUMI/DexCap) no force/tactile** — the one signal video cannot fake. See [Data foundry](data-foundry.md) and [Tactile & hands](tactile-hands.md). *(mixed; wearables corpus figures company-reported)*

## 中国数据采集生态 (China Data-Collection Ecosystem)

> China is a *structurally distinct* data-collection story, not just a cheaper copy of the Western one. Three forces stack: (1) **state-backed 数据训练场** (national/municipal "training grounds" that socialize collection cost across government, hardware vendors, and cheap labor — 40+ announced, ~two dozen operational as of 2026-07); (2) **cheap teleop labor** (JD gig home-collection documented at **20 yuan / $3 per hour**; only the $3 gig figure is source-confirmed — broader "$3–20/h" ranges are cited loosely; vs $20–50/h US — see [Data foundry](data-foundry.md)); and (3) **unmatched hardware-vendor density** — the same supply chain that makes 63–70% of the world's humanoid components (see [Landscape: China](landscape-china.md)) also cost-reduces the *collection rigs themselves* (AgileX turned Mobile-ALOHA into a €16k catalog SKU; a dozen vendors now sell purpose-built data-collection gloves/grippers/egocentric kits). The result is a market where the ALOHA/UMI/DexCap designs from the [device matrix](data-collection-devices.md#1-taxonomy--tradeoff-table) reappear as *commodity Chinese products*, and where open dataset releases (AgiBot World, Galaxea Open-World, RoboMIND) are used as ecosystem subsidies the way Chinese LLM labs use open weights. This section covers the Chinese *players and their rigs*; for device physics see [data-collection-devices](data-collection-devices.md), for facility economics see [data-foundry](data-foundry.md), for company/funding context see [landscape-china](landscape-china.md) and [company-agibot](company-agibot.md).

It is a **dedicated data-collection *device* vendor**, product-lined as **DAS Ego** (first-person/egocentric camera rig), **DAS Controller** (isomorphic teleoperation controller), **DAS Gripper** (handheld UMI-class gripper), **DAS Dex** (dexterous), and **DAS Fingers** (tactile sensor fingers), plus a **Gen Matrix** data-governance platform; captures 头+手+全身 (head/hands/full-body) multimodal data across home/factory/logistics, claiming 10,000+ hours over 50+ scenarios. **Critical disambiguation:** do **not** confuse it with 智元 AgiBot's "**Genie Studio / Genie Operator (GO-1)**" one-stop development platform (zhiyuan-robot.com) — the names collide in search but they are different entities (GenRobot = independent Beijing rig vendor; Genie = AgiBot's software platform + model line). GenRobot is best understood as a Chinese "arms dealer" selling the *entire* family-A/B/C device taxonomy from the [device matrix](data-collection-devices.md#1-taxonomy--tradeoff-table) as off-the-shelf SKUs.

### 读数 (Synthesis) — how China's data-collection ecosystem differs from the West

- **Structural difference #1 — who pays.** The West splits into UMI-class *academic* rigs (Stanford/Columbia/NYU printing $371 grippers) and *private* teleop fleets (PI staged Airbnbs, Figure/Tesla operators, Generalist wearables — see [data-foundry](data-foundry.md)). China adds a **third payer the West lacks: the state.** 40+ 数据训练场 socialize collection cost across MIIT/municipal budgets, hardware vendors (who supply robots at a discount to seed centers), and cheap labor (JD gig home-collection **$3/h** confirmed; training-center operator hourly wages are not disclosed in the cited reporting — the "$5–20/h" range is *unverified*). The MIIT+SASAC 实景实训 special action (2026-06-09) effectively converts *every mandated deployment scenario into a data-collection site* — a state purchase order for foundry capacity ([landscape-china](landscape-china.md)).
- **Structural difference #2 — hardware-vendor cost-down.** The West's family-A rigs are *open-source BOMs* (anyone prints them); China *productizes* them. AgileX ships Mobile-ALOHA as a €16k catalog SKU; GenRobot/它石/帕西尼 sell egocentric/teleop/tactile kits as turnkey products. The same 60–70% component supply-chain dominance that cost-reduces humanoids ([landscape-china](landscape-china.md)) cost-reduces the *rigs*. Net: China's comparative advantage is **cheap, integrated, buyable collection hardware at volume** — the "arms-dealer" wedge the [device matrix §4](data-collection-devices.md#4-读数-synthesis--the-core-tension) flags, realized.
- **Structural difference #3 — heterogeneous cross-vendor data.** Because state training grounds run *100+ robot models from different makers (~10 vendors at Zhangjiang) on the same scenarios*, China uniquely generates **cross-embodiment** data at facility scale (国地中心 "heterogeneous" ground, X-Humanoid 120+ models). Western fleets are mostly single-embodiment (Figure→Figure, PI→its own robots). Whether heterogeneous data actually trains better cross-embodiment policies is unproven — but it's a bet only the state-coordinated model can place.
- **Open vs closed.** China is the *open-data counterweight*: AgiBot World (1M+ traj), Galaxea Open-World (100k), RoboMIND 2.0, 它石 WIYH are all open releases used as ecosystem subsidies — mirroring Chinese open-weight LLM strategy. Yet the *frontier* Chinese corpora stay proprietary too (AgiBot's live firehose, Spirit AI's 1,000-device network, Galbot's real-store data). The honest split: **China open-sources curated slices to seed the ecosystem; the West's leaders (Generalist 500k h, PI, Figure) open-source nothing.** The largest *open* real-robot corpus is Chinese; the largest corpus *period* (Generalist, proprietary) is Western — see [data-foundry](data-foundry.md) proprietary-vs-open gap.
- **The honest verdict.** China's edge is **cost structure and coordination, not data quality or verifiability.** The three most *rigorously documented* Chinese efforts (Galaxea, AgiBot World, ByteDance GR-3) all have real papers/dataset cards and are genuinely competitive. But the *tier below* — Spirit AI's "1,000 devices/100 cities," LimX's "Data Recipe," Noematrix's "CoMiner," JD's "10M hours," and every training-ground "10M records" target — is **company/state-reported, single-source, and often a plan or a target rather than a delivered, curated, verifiable dataset.** The 2026 embodied-AI funding froth ([landscape-china](landscape-china.md)) incentivizes headline scale numbers over disclosed methodology. Read Chinese collection claims the way you read Chinese humanoid shipment claims: the *direction* (state-scale, cheap, hardware-dense, open-slice) is real and structurally advantaged; the *specific magnitudes* need primary-source verification. The durable Chinese moat is not any single dataset — it is the **vertically integrated stack** (own robot + own rig vendor + state-subsidized facility + cheap labor + open-slice flywheel) that no Western player can replicate at the same cost.

## 4. 读数 (Synthesis) — the core tension

- **The fundamental tradeoff is not going away.** Embodiment-free rigs (UMI-class) are cheap ($25–$400), fast (~111 demos/h, ~3× teleop), and crowd-scalable — but they carry an **embodiment gap** (a handheld gripper/hand/walking-human is not the deployment robot's arm/hand/base) and **recovered, not measured, action labels** (SLAM/VIO/ARKit estimates the pose → 6mm–24mm error, drift, and failure modes enter the training data). Teleop rigs (ALOHA/DROID/AgiBot) give **exact measured joint actions** on (or morphologically near) the deployment robot — but cost ~$22k/station (stationary ALOHA 2; ~$32k only with a mobile base) plus a human, and hit a hard **~35 demos/h operator-fatigue ceiling** (iDP3's authors state plainly that AVP teleop is "tiring for human teleoperators, making imitation data hard to scale up"). You cannot have cheap-and-scalable *and* clean-and-zero-gap from one device.
- **Where each is winning in 2026-07:** family A (robot-free/human) is winning the **pretraining scale** war — Generalist 500k+ h, Figure Go-Big, NVIDIA EgoScale's log-linear dexterity law all say cheap human data at volume moves the needle. Family B/C (teleop/on-robot) is winning the **last mile** — reliability, contact-rich, force-critical tasks still need clean labels, and RL-from-teleop-corrections (π*0.6/RECAP) is the current best reliability lever. The field has converged on **pretrain-cheap, fine-tune-clean hybrids**; no serious lab bets on one family.
- **The trend line:** the device count in family A is exploding (UMI → FastUMI → DexUMI → ActiveUMI → Mobile-UMI → HoMMI → Dobb·E → DexCap → Generalist/Genesis gloves), each variant chipping at a specific limit — mirrors (depth), T265 (calibration), exo+inpainting (dexterous gap), chest-cam (mobile base), gloves (tactile+dexterity). But every variant that adds capability (dexterous hands, mobile bases) **widens** the embodiment gap it must then patch, which is why the hard problems (force, exact kinematics, human-gait→wheeled-base) recur. Teleop is not being replaced; it is being **relegated to the last mile** where labels must be exact.
- **The ODM / hardware-supplier read** ([Quectel-style "arms dealer" wedge](data-foundry.md)): there **is** a real hardware-supplier opportunity, but it is a **low-margin, volume niche**, not a moat.
  - *The bull case:* every lab and every one of China's 40+ state data centers needs physical rigs — grippers, wrist-cam mounts, RealSense/T265 modules, capture cards, mocap gloves, leader-follower arms, tactile e-skin. UMI/Fast-UMI/Dobb·E are literally **BOMs of commodity parts** (3D-printed gripper, GoPro, RealSense, iPhone, ARX/Piper arms, Rokoko gloves). A module maker that ships a **calibrated, ROS-ready, quality-controlled capture kit** (the thing labs actually struggle with — per-device IMU-cam calibration, drift QA, LeRobot-format output) sells into a market that is scaling with the entire data-foundry buildout.
  - *The bear case:* the winning rigs are **deliberately cheap and open-source** — UMI is $371 of parts anyone can print; the whole point is commodity access. Margins live in **integration and QA** (calibration, drift detection, format compliance), not in the parts. The value is migrating from *hardware* to *the data itself* (the corpus is the moat, not the rig — see [Data foundry](data-foundry.md)), so a pure rig supplier is selling picks-and-shovels in a gold rush where the picks cost $25. The durable play is **tactile/sensor modules** (the one thing family-A rigs mostly lack and can't fake — force is not in video), **precision tracking modules** (the fidelity bottleneck), and **calibrated turnkey kits** for the volume centers — a components-and-integration business, not a platform business.
  - *The tell:* the sensor modules that matter most (RealSense T265/L515, GoPro IMU, ZED stereo, Rokoko EMF, tactile e-skin) are exactly where an established **module-maker** already has manufacturing scale — the wedge is supplying *the sensing subsystem of the rig*, not the rig.

## 5. Watch items

- **Tactile-in-the-rig**: force/tactile is the one signal video-based family-A rigs structurally cannot capture (DexUMI, DexCap-EMF, Genesis glove are early answers). Whoever makes cheap, robust, rig-integrable tactile skin unlocks contact-rich data at scale. See [Manipulation](manipulation.md) tactile section.
- **Tracking-fidelity floor**: UMI's SLAM (6mm) vs FastUMI's T265 (24mm) vs ARKit (black-box) — the position-error floor of cheap tracking bounds how good robot-free labels can be. Watch VIO/SLAM module improvements.
- **Mobile-base gap**: Mobile-UMI / HoMMI / UMI-on-Legs all attack human-locomotion → robot-base transfer differently; none is verified/replicated. Which approach (sim WBC vs direct SE(2) recovery) wins is open.
- **Dexterous-hand gap closure**: does the Genesis 1:1:1 glove or DexUMI's exo+inpainting actually close the human-hand → robot-hand gap, or is it patched-not-solved? All current evidence is single-source/company-reported.
- **Rig commoditization vs QA moat**: does a "calibrated capture kit" business emerge, or do labs keep printing their own $371 rigs and pushing value entirely to the corpus?
