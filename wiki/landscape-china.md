> China is the volume center of the humanoid-robot industry: roughly 12,800 of the world's humanoid units were produced there in 2025 (~90% of global output, per Merics), led by Unitree (5,500 humanoids sold in 2025; STAR Market IPO registration approved July 2026 at a ~$6–6.2B target valuation) and AgiBot/Zhiyuan (5,168 shipped in 2025 per Omdia; 15,000th cumulative unit by June 2026). The ecosystem combines a 60–70% share of the global component supply chain, aggressive price disruption (Unitree's R1 at ~$5,900), state policy that made embodied intelligence a named priority of the 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–30), and high-visibility public milestones — a robot half-marathon whose 2026 winner beat the human world record, and the first World Humanoid Robot Games (Beijing, Aug 2025). Contrast with [Landscape: USA](landscape-usa.md); see [Humanoid robots](humanoid-robots.md) for hardware detail and [Investment](investment.md) for capital flows.

## Why China leads on volume

- **Scale**: ~12,800 humanoid units produced in China in 2025 — roughly 90% of global output (Merics, citing industry data; as of 2026-05). NDRC counts ~150 Chinese firms building humanoid robots (other counts run to ~160).
- **Supply chain**: Chinese suppliers hold ~63–70% of the global humanoid component supply chain (actuators, reducers, motors, sensors, batteries), with an estimated 30–40% cost advantage. China controls ~69% of rare-earth mining and ~90% of magnet processing — critical for the NdFeB magnets in high-torque actuators. See [Hardware](hardware.md).
- **Capital**: embodied-intelligence financing in China reached RMB 73.5B (~$10.8B) in 2025; a national venture guidance fund plans ~RMB 1 trillion for AI/robotics/frontier tech over ~20 years.
- **Policy**: embodied AI entered the 2025 Government Work Report and the 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030) as a top "new industry track"; MIIT stood up a humanoid/embodied-AI standardization committee (Dec 2025) and released a first national standards system for the humanoid industry (Mar 2026).
- **Demand-side pull**: auto OEMs (BYD, Geely, FAW-VW, Audi FAW, Dongfeng), electronics manufacturers (Foxconn), and logistics firms (SF Express) are piloting humanoids in factories, giving Chinese vendors deployment data US rivals mostly lack. See [Data](data.md).
- **Caveat**: most 2025 unit volume was research/education units and small pilots, not sustained industrial labor. Unitree's own prospectus says >70% of its humanoid sales went to research and educational buyers.

## Unitree

- Founded 2016 (Hangzhou) by Wang Xingxing; began in quadrupeds, now the highest-revenue pure-play legged-robot maker. See [Key people](key-people.md).
- **Financials** (IPO prospectus, as of 2026-03): revenue RMB 392M in 2024 → RMB 1.71B (~$250M) in 2025; adjusted net profit ~RMB 600M (~$90M) in 2025, up 674% YoY — rare profitability in this sector. 480 employees, 175 in R&D.
- **Volumes**: 5,500 humanoids sold in 2025; >30,000 quadrupeds shipped 2022–2025-09. Prospectus claims 32.4% of global 2025 humanoid unit shipments (see share-count caveat below). Plans capacity of 75,000 humanoids + 115,000 quadrupeds/yr within five years.
- **Price trajectory**: average humanoid selling price fell from ~RMB 593,400 (~$85k) in 2023 to ~RMB 167,600 (~$25k) in 2025, a ~72% drop, while gross margin rose to ~60%. R1 launched 2025-07-25 at RMB 39,900 (~$5,900): 1.21 m, 25 kg, 26 DoF, hot-swappable battery, onboard multimodal model (UnifoLM).
- **Capital markets**: June 2025 Series C at RMB 12.7B (~$1.7B) post-money (Alibaba, Tencent, China Mobile, Ant Group, Geely). Filed for Shanghai STAR Market IPO 2026-03-20 seeking RMB 4.2B (~$610–620M); listing committee approved 2026-06-01 in a record 73 days — first "embodied AI" company cleared for A-shares; CSRC registration approved 2026-07 ($618M raise); target post-IPO valuation ~RMB 42B (~$6–6.2B).
- Dominated the first World Humanoid Robot Games (2025-08): 11 medals incl. 4 golds (400 m, 1,500 m, 100 m hurdles, 4×100 m relay); its H1 hit 4.78 m/s in competition. See [Locomotion](locomotion.md).

## AgiBot / Zhiyuan Robotics

- Founded 2023-02 (Shanghai) by Peng Zhihui ("Zhihui Jun," ex-Huawei "genius youth") and others; fastest scale-up in the sector.
- **Volumes**: shipped 5,168 humanoids in 2025 — 39% of the global market per Omdia, which puts total 2025 global shipments at ~13,000 (conflicts with Unitree's 32.4% self-claim; the two counts likely use different category boundaries — both "(competing-methodology)" claims). Cumulative 10,000th unit rolled out 2026-03-30; 15,000th (a G2 industrial unit) followed 2026-06-28 — the 5,000→10,000 step took ~3 months.
- **Models/data**: released GO-1 generalist embodied foundation model 2025-03, trained on >1M robot trajectories across 217 tasks (AgiBot World dataset — one of the largest open real-robot manipulation corpora); GO-2 followed in April 2026 (unverified). See [VLA models](vla-models.md) and [Data](data.md).
- **Capital**: strategic round 2025-08 with LG Electronics and Mirae Asset (amount undisclosed; LG's first embodied-AI bet), plus BYD and Hillhouse earlier; valuation reported >RMB 15B mid-2025. Acquired ~63.6% control of A-share-listed Swancor Advanced Materials for ~RMB 2.1B (~$290M) in July 2025 — denied it was a backdoor listing; Swancor stock hit an 11-day limit-up streak. Reported HK IPO target Q3 2026 at HK$40–50B ($5.1–6.4B) (unverified).

## UBTech

- Shenzhen; listed on HKEX (9880) Dec 2023 — the earliest publicly traded Chinese humanoid pure-play.
- **2025 results** (reported 2026-03-31): revenue RMB 2.001B (+53.3% YoY); full-size humanoid segment RMB 820M, up 2,203.7% YoY, on 1,079 units delivered — first company to exceed 1,000 full-size humanoid deliveries in a year. Gross margin 37.7%; net loss narrowed ~32% to RMB 790M (still loss-making).
- **Walker S2** (industrial humanoid, autonomous hot-swap battery): mass production and first several-hundred-unit batch delivery began 2025-11; cumulative Walker-series orders >RMB 800M (~$112M) since early 2025, incl. a RMB 250M order (2025-09) and RMB 159M for a data-collection center in Zigong. Capacity plan: 5,000 units/yr by 2026, 10,000 by 2027.
- Customers/pilots: BYD, Foxconn, Geely, FAW-Volkswagen Qingdao, Audi FAW, BAIC New Energy, Dongfeng Liuzhou, SF Express — mostly automotive final-assembly, logistics, and data-collection roles.

## Galbot

- Beijing; founded 2023-05, spun out of Peking University robotics research (founder Wang He). Product is the **G1 semi-humanoid** — wheeled base + dual arms, optimized for retail picking rather than legs.
- **Deployment**: runs fully autonomous "Galbot Store" convenience stores — claimed world's first humanoid-operated store in Beijing — expanded to 30+ cities, plus 24/7 robot-staffed pharmacies in Beijing (10+). One of the few Chinese players with recurring commercial operations rather than hardware sales.
- **Capital** (as of 2025-12): raised >$300M in a round valuing it at $3B (record for China's humanoid sector at the time); total funding ~$800M. Earlier 2025: RMB 1.1B (~$153M) led by CATL and Puquan Capital; a ~$151M round involving Bosch's investment arm (2025-07). Picked CITIC Securities, Huatai, and UBS for a planned 2026 Hong Kong IPO (Bloomberg, 2025-12).
- Won a gold medal at the 2025 World Humanoid Robot Games (retail/service scenario events).

## Fourier (Fourier Intelligence)

- Shanghai; founded 2015 in rehabilitation robotics; pivoted to humanoids with GR-1 (2023) and GR-2 (2024).
- **GR-3** (unveiled 2025-08): first "care-bot" positioning — 165 cm, 71 kg, 55 DoF, soft-touch exterior, 31 pressure sensors, full-sensory (audio/visual/tactile) interaction, emotion recognition; ~$27,500 B2B price in China (as of 2025-08); ~3 h battery.
- Strategy is differentiated: healthcare, rehab, and eldercare rather than factory labor — a bet on China's aging demographics.

## EngineAI

- Shenzhen; founded late 2023 by ex-XPeng robotics engineers (CEO Zhao Tongyang).
- **Milestone**: world's first humanoid robot front flip (PM01, 2025-02) — part of a wave of Chinese acrobatic-locomotion demos.
- **Price disruption**: PM01 commercial version at RMB 88,000 (~$12k); 1.38 m, ~40 kg, 23–24 DoF.
- **Capital**: ~RMB 1B (~$139M) raised across pre-A++ (Rocket Capital, XPeng) and A1 (JD.com, CATL Capital, Yintai, Tsinghua Holdings) rounds announced 2025-07; A1+/A2 rounds completed by 2025-12 alongside launch of full-size T800 (factory claims one T800 every 15 minutes (unverified)). Filed confidentially for a Hong Kong IPO 2026-06 (Bloomberg).

## Xiaomi and XPeng (consumer-electronics / EV entrants)

- **Xiaomi**: unveiled CyberOne in 2022; showed **CyberOne V2** at a 2026 investor day doing social/interaction tasks after claiming a 90.2% success rate in autonomous automotive-assembly pilot tasks (company figure, unverified). Open-sourced the "Xiaomi-Robotics-0" embodied model in 2026 (unverified). No public specs, price, or mass-production timeline as of 2026-07.
- **XPeng**: unveiled next-gen **IRON** at AI Day 2025-11-05 — 82 DoF (22 per hand), humanoid spine + bionic muscle + flexible skin, 3× in-house Turing chips (~3,000 TOPS), first robot with an all-solid-state battery; runs a "VLT+VLA+VLM" stack sharing XPeng's automotive VLA 2.0 lineage. The catwalk demo was so smooth it triggered viral "person in a suit" accusations, which XPeng rebutted by cutting the leg covering on camera. Factory groundbreaking Q1 2026; mass production targeted end-2026, initial roles in showrooms, museums, malls; CEO He Xiaopeng has floated ~1M units/yr ambition by 2030 (aspirational). XPeng is also an EngineAI investor.
- Automakers-as-robot-makers is a distinct Chinese pattern (XPeng, Xiaomi, GAC, Chery, BYD as investor/customer): they bring EV supply chains, mass-manufacturing muscle, and in-house AI chips. See [Organizations](organizations.md).

## Government policy and subsidies

- **Timeline**:
  - 2023-11: MIIT "Guiding Opinions on Innovative Development of Humanoid Robots" — mass production by 2025, world-leading industry by 2027.
  - 2025-03: embodied AI named in the national Government Work Report for the first time; "AI+" strategy frames robots as intelligent terminals for the whole economy.
  - 2025-12: MIIT establishes Humanoid Robot & Embodied Intelligence Standardization Technical Committee.
  - 2026-03: 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030) designates robotics a strategic emerging industry and embodied intelligence a top-ten "new industry track"; first national humanoid standards system released.
- **Money** (as of 2026-04): ~RMB 1 trillion (~$137B) national VC guidance fund for AI/robotics over two decades; Shenzhen RMB 10B AI & robotics fund (2025); Beijing Robot Industry Fund (a Galbot investor); local subsidies up to 30% of automation project costs plus purchase discounts for humanoids; estimates of ~$20B in direct subsidies to the sector (single-source, unverified).
- **State demand**: government-linked buyers (data-collection centers, state utilities, municipal pilots) are a meaningful share of early orders — e.g., UBTech's RMB 159M Zigong data-collection-center order.
- Analysts (Merics, East Asia Forum) frame the policy as deliberately replaying the EV playbook: subsidize supply, seed demand, let brutal domestic price competition select winners, then export.

## Supply-chain advantage

- Actuators + motion systems are the dominant humanoid cost center (estimates range ~40–70% of BOM depending on design); China makes most of the world's harmonic/planetary reducers, frameless motors, and NdFeB magnets at scale.
- One analysis estimates building Tesla's Optimus Gen 2 without Chinese suppliers would raise BOM from ~$46k to ~$131k (~3×) (single-source, unverified). See [Landscape: USA](landscape-usa.md) for the mirror-image dependency problem.
- Yangtze River Delta (Hangzhou/Shanghai/Suzhou) hosts a vertically integrated cluster; Unitree builds motors, reducers, and sensors in-house with suppliers within a ~2-hour logistics radius. The Pearl River Delta (Shenzhen) cluster hosts UBTech, EngineAI, Honor and the electronics supply base.
- Rare-earth export controls (2025) demonstrated leverage: humanoid-grade actuators depend on Chinese magnet processing (~90% global share).
- Goldman Sachs notes Chinese component suppliers are building humanoid capacity ahead of confirmed orders — capacity overshoot risk, but also a structural moat if demand arrives.

## Price-disruption dynamics

| Robot | Vendor | Launch | Price (as of launch) |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 (full-size) | Unitree | 2023 | ~$90k |
| G1 (1.32 m) | Unitree | 2024-05 | RMB 99k (~$16k) |
| PM01 (1.38 m) | EngineAI | 2024-12 | RMB 88k (~$12k) |
| R1 (1.21 m) | Unitree | 2025-07 | RMB 39,900 (~$5,900); R1 Air repriced to RMB 29,900 (~$4,200; ~$4,900 international) 2026-06 |
| GR-3 (care) | Fourier | 2025-08 | ~$27.5k B2B |

- Unitree's fleet-average humanoid price fell ~70% in two years ($85k → $25k, 2023→2025) while the company became profitable — evidence the cost curve, not subsidy alone, is driving prices down.
- Dynamics mirror the Chinese EV/solar/drone playbook: dozens of funded entrants, component commoditization, sub-$10k entry hardware expanding the developer/education market, thin margins forcing consolidation. Expect a shakeout among the ~150 humanoid firms; IPO proceeds (Unitree, UBTech, Galbot, EngineAI, AgiBot) are the war chests.
- Open question: cheap hardware ≠ useful labor. Most sub-$20k units are R&D/education/performance platforms; industrial units (Walker S2) still sell for six figures and rely on structured tasks. The binding constraint is autonomy software and data, not price. See [Open problems](open-problems.md).

## Cross-cutting caveats

- **Conflicting market-share numbers**: Unitree claims 32.4% of 2025 global humanoid shipments (prospectus); Omdia credits AgiBot with 39% on 5,168 units against a ~13,000-unit global total — a denominator under which Unitree's 5,500 units would be ~42%, not 32.4%. With Merics separately putting Chinese production at ~12,800 units (~90% of global), the counts cannot share a methodology — treat all 2025 share figures as methodology-dependent.
- Company-reported delivery, success-rate, and capacity numbers (UBTech targets, Xiaomi 90.2%, EngineAI 15-min takt) are largely unaudited marketing claims until IPO prospectuses force disclosure.
- Chinese "delivery" figures often include sales to research institutes, rental/performance fleets, and state-linked data-collection centers — not equivalent to sustained industrial deployment hours.
