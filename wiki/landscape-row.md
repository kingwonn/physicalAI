> Outside the [US](landscape-usa.md) and [China](landscape-china.md), Physical AI is led by a handful of well-capitalized European startups (1X, NEURA Robotics, Wandercraft), Japan's industrial-robotics incumbents pivoting toward AI plus a state-backed foundation-model consortium, and Korea's chaebol conglomerates (Samsung, LG, Hyundai, Doosan) buying and building their way into humanoids. As of 2026-06 the region's headline events are NEURA's up-to-$1.4B Series C (Europe's largest robotics round), Hyundai taking 100% ownership of Boston Dynamics, Japan's SoftBank–NEC–Sony–Honda "Japan AI Foundation Model Development" JV (operating company: **Noetra**) with up to ¥1T in state backing, Korea's ">$1 trillion" triple-axis AI investment drive (announced 2026-06-29: chips + AI data centers + physical AI), and Mobileye's $900M acquisition of Israel's Mentee Robotics.

### 1X Technologies (Norway/US)
- Founded 2014 as Halodi Robotics (Moss, Norway); OpenAI Startup Fund, EQT Ventures and Tiger Global among backers. NEO Beta unveiled 2024-08; NEO Gamma 2025-02.
- Opened NEO pre-orders 2025-10: **$20,000 purchase or $499/month subscription** ($200 deposit); company reported ~10,000 pre-orders in the first five days (company figure, unverified).
- Struck a deal (2025-12) to also deploy its "home" humanoids into factories and warehouses — hedging the consumer bet with industrial revenue.
- Began **full-scale NEO production 2026-04** at a 58,000 sq ft factory in Hayward, California (200+ staff, as of 2026-04).
- NEO hardware: ~30 kg soft-bodied frame (vs ~57 kg Tesla Optimus), 22-DoF hands, tendon-driven actuation; autonomy backstopped by human teleoperation for chores it can't yet do — see [Humanoid robots](humanoid-robots.md) and [Data](data.md) on the teleop-as-data-flywheel strategy.
- Reported (2025-09) to be raising up to **$1B at a ≥$10B valuation**, up from ~$820M after its 2024 $100M Series B (press reports, unverified; no closed round confirmed as of 2026-07).

### NEURA Robotics (Germany)
- Founded 2019 (David Reger); "cognitive robotics" full-stack: 4NE1 humanoid, MAiRA cognitive cobot arm, MiPA household/mobile manipulator, plus the **Neuraverse** shared robot-learning ecosystem and "NEURA Gyms" data-collection facilities.
- **Series C of up to $1.4B announced 2026-06-10** — the largest round ever for a European robotics company. Lead: Tether; participants include Qualcomm, Amazon, NVIDIA, Bosch, Schaeffler, imec.xpand, Lingotto, InterAlpen and the European Investment Bank. Reported valuation ~$7B (Bloomberg/CNBC reports; not company-confirmed). Round is milestone-contingent — the full "up to $1.4B" depends on **undisclosed performance milestones**; it is a ceiling, not cash banked. Prior round: €120M Series B, 2025-01, led by Lingotto.
- Orderbook and deployment pipeline stated at **>$1B (as of 2026-06)**; large-scale 4NE1 shipments targeted from late 2026; stated goal of scaling manufacturing across **Germany and India** toward "millions of robots by 2030" (company claims).

### Wandercraft (France)
- Exoskeleton maker (Atalante X rehab system; Eve self-balancing personal exoskeleton targeted for market from 2026) that pivoted its dynamic-walking expertise into industrial humanoids.
- **Calvin-40** industrial humanoid developed in 40 days with Renault Group; Renault holds a minority stake. Series D of **$75M (equity+debt), 2025-06**, incl. Renault, France 2030/Bpifrance PSIM fund.
- Deployment roadmap: ~10 Calvins in Renault plants by end-2026, **350 robots across French and Spanish factories by end-2027** (plan, as of 2025-06). Calvin is headless by design — an early data point that industrial buyers don't pay for anthropomorphism.

### PAL Robotics (Spain)
- Founded 2004; Europe's longest-running humanoid vendor (REEM, TALOS research humanoid, ARI, TIAGo family).
- **Kangaroo** bipedal lower-body/humanoid moved from prototype to purchasable product in 2025; shown at Automatica, IROS 2025 and Humanoids 2025 (Seoul).
- New AI-ready robotic arm platform announced 2026-05, to be fully unveiled at ICRA 2026 (Vienna) with sales later in 2026. Primarily a research/EU-project-funded business rather than a venture-scale humanoid bet.

### Humanoid (UK)
- London startup founded 2024 by Artem Sokolov; ~$50M founder-led capital and ~175 staff (as of 2025-09, company figures).
- **HMND 01 Alpha** wheeled dual-arm mobile manipulator unveiled 2025-09 (built in 7 months): 220 cm reach envelope, 15 kg two-arm payload, 7.2 km/h; RaaS business model. Beta planned Q3 2026; a bipedal variant is on the roadmap (unverified).

### Europe — reading the region
- Europe's strength is industrial demand-side pull (automotive OEMs like Renault, Bosch/Schaeffler as strategic investors) and EU/state co-investment (EIB, France 2030), not consumer robots — 1X is the exception and it relocated manufacturing to California.
- No European company yet trains frontier-scale [VLA models](vla-models.md) in-house at US/China scale; NEURA's Neuraverse is the most explicit attempt at a European data/skill ecosystem. See [Investment](investment.md) for round-size comparisons.
- Europe's largest **closed** physical-AI rounds of 2026 are not humanoid makers: **Wayve** (London, autonomous driving / embodied AI) closed a $1.2B Series D at $8.6B post-money on 2026-02-25 (plus $300M milestone-based Uber capital, $1.5B total secured), and **AMI Labs** (Paris, Yann LeCun's world-model lab) closed a $1.03B seed at $3.5B pre-money on 2026-03-09 — the largest European seed round on record. Contrast with NEURA's milestone-contingent "up to $1.4B". See [Investment](investment.md) and [World models](world-models.md).

## Japan

### National strategy & the foundation-model JV
- **"Japan AI Foundation Model Development" JV announced 2026-04-13**: SoftBank Corp., NEC, Sony Group and Honda, with backing from Nippon Steel, Kobe Steel and three major banks; up to **¥1 trillion (~$6.1–6.3B) in NEDO funding over five years** to build a domestically trained, trillion-parameter foundation model aimed at Physical AI (robotics, AVs, factory automation).
- The operating company is now named **Noetra**; on **2026-06-30** NEDO formally selected the Noetra–AIST proposal under its "Multimodal Foundation Models for AI Robots and Physical AI" program, with **¥387.3B (~$2.4B) in FY2026 subsidies** as the first tranche of the up-to-¥1T five-year total.
- Roles: SoftBank + NEC train the models; Sony + Honda handle deployment into sensors, vehicles and robots. Honda is reported to contribute >2.3M hours of teleoperated driving data plus ASIMO-lineage manipulation traces; Sony contributes image-sensor datasets (press reports, unverified).
- Japan's national robotics strategy (announced **2026-07-01**) targets **~10 million AI-powered robots deployed across 18 industries by 2040**, explicitly framed as an answer to labor shortage. See [World models](world-models.md) and [VLA models](vla-models.md) for what such a model would need.

### Toyota Research Institute (TRI)
- TRI is Japan's strongest robot-learning lab, home of the **Large Behavior Model (LBM)** research program (diffusion-policy multitask manipulation; Russ Tedrake VP of Robotics Research) — see [State of the art](state-of-the-art.md).
- **2024-10**: joint research partnership with Boston Dynamics (co-led by Tedrake and Scott Kuindersma). **2025-08-20**: first public demo of an LBM driving Atlas — a ~450M-parameter Diffusion-Transformer / flow-matching policy producing whole-body manipulation + locomotion (walking, crouching, lifting) in long continuous sequences with no task-specific programming.
- Toyota is also an early external trial user of Rainbow Robotics' RB-Y1 (as of 2026-06, single report, unverified).

### Industrial incumbents
| Company | Humanoid/AI posture (as of 2026-06) |
|---|---|
| FANUC | No humanoid; showing LLM/plain-language cobot programming (Automate 2026); dominant in CNC + industrial arms |
| Yaskawa | **Acquired Tokyo Robotics (100%, announced 2025-10)** — Torobo wheeled dual-arm humanoid (Waseda spinout, torque sensing/impedance control); MOTOMAN NEXT adaptive cells on NVIDIA stack |
| Kawasaki Heavy Industries | **Kaleido gen-9** humanoid shown at iREX 2025; first pilots targeted 2026 |
| Honda | ASIMO line retired as a product; robotics know-how folded into the ASIMO OS brand (0-series EVs) and the 2026 foundation-model JV |

- The strategic picture: Japan retains the world's densest industrial-robot supply chain (FANUC, Yaskawa, Kawasaki, plus servo/reducer suppliers — see [Hardware](hardware.md)) but has **no venture-scale humanoid champion**; Chinese humanoid vendors began actively selling into Japan in 2025–26 (see the JAL Haneda trial below). Japan's bet is sovereign models + incumbents' manufacturing rather than startups.

### JAL × GMO AI & Robotics: humanoid trial at Haneda
- **Japan Airlines (via ops subsidiary JAL Ground Service) + GMO AI & Robotics** (GMO Internet Group's AI/robotics unit) announced 2026-04 what JAL calls **Japan's first airport humanoid-robot demonstration**: trials at Tokyo Haneda running **2026-05 through 2028**, with robots deployed progressively across the airport.
- Initial phase: two units on container transport and opening/closing container-securing levers. Target tasks: **baggage/cargo loading, ground-support-equipment (GSE) operation, and cabin cleaning**; human supervisors retain control of safety functions. Framing is explicit labor-shortage relief — record inbound tourism against a shrinking aviation workforce.
- Hardware is **Unitree G1-based** (~1.32 m, ~35 kg, ~2 h per charge) — a Chinese platform working inside Japan's flagship airport, the concrete case of the Chinese-vendors-into-Japan dynamic above. Per-unit cost is reported inconsistently: **~$13.5K (base-variant figure per New Atlas) vs ~$15.4K (per CNBC)**. See [Humanoid robots](humanoid-robots.md) for G1 platform detail.

## South Korea

### National AI strategy: the ">$1T triple axis" (2026-06-29)
- **President Lee Jae Myung announced (2026-06-29)**, at the "3 Mega-Projects for Korea's Great Leap" national briefing (대한민국 대도약 3대 메가프로젝트 국민보고회, held at the presidential guest house 영빈관), a public-private investment drive totaling **more than $1 trillion**: "Semiconductors, physical AI, and AI data centers are the triple axis for our great leap forward." Won figures below are **primary-verified** against the Industry Ministry (산업통상부, MOTIE) press release of 2026-06-29 and korea.kr policy briefings; Industry Minister Kim Jung-kwan presented the "3S+1F" strategy.
- Semiconductors: **₩800T** — Samsung + SK (with suppliers) build **four new fabs (two per company, ~₩400T each) plus supplier ecosystem in the southwest**; exact fab sites were **still undecided as of 2026-07** — Gwangju (Cheomdan-3 district or the military-airport site) is the leading candidate for Samsung's, South Jeolla sites (Jangseong/Haenam/Muan) for SK's (per Financial News Korea) — alongside accelerated Seoul-region fab completion (timelines shortened 7–12 years) — plus **₩81T** for a Chungcheong packaging hub (new HBM fabs in Onyang/Cheonan, HBM packaging in Cheongju), and ₩30T+ over 15 years for next-gen semiconductor R&D. Stated goal: **double memory (DRAM) production capacity within five years**.
  - **Dollar-figure reconciliation**: the apparent $518B-vs-$585B conflict is a **scope difference, not a factual one**. Al Jazeera/AP's **$518B = the ₩800T southwest-fab component alone**; Ars Technica (FT syndication) **$585B**, CNN/Reuters **$576B**, and Korea Herald's **$583B** (the paper's own conversion — the government stated won figures only) all ≈ the **combined ₩881T** (800T fabs + 81T packaging). Residual spread is won-conversion noise (implied rates ~₩1,505–1,545/$).
- AI data centers: **₩550T** from SK Group, GS Group and Naver — stage 1 of **8.4 GW** (SK 5 GW, GS 2.4 GW, Naver 1 GW), SK expanding to 15 GW by 2035, **18.4 GW total planned**. (Al Jazeera's "₩1,000T by 2035" scaling figure does not appear in the MOTIE release — unverified against primary.)
- Physical AI: the MOTIE release calls physical AI/humanoids an **"irreplaceable national strategic industry" (대체 불가능한 국가전략산업)** — framing language in the release, not (so far as documented) a formal statutory designation under Korea's national high-tech strategic industry law. Concrete goals — primary-verified in the MOTIE release text: **1,000+ AI robots deployed into worksites annually** (start year not stated in the release; 2027 per ZDNet Korea's "from next year"), **10,000 AI-robotics specialists trained over five years**, **data factories for ten industries** (10대 업종별 데이터팩토리) + a sovereign **robot foundation model (RFM) within three years**, and a **"global top-1 in physical AI by 2030"** target. Reported from the briefing but *not* in the release text: **10 industry-specialized humanoids commercialized by 2028** (Korea Herald, ZDNet Korea, Ars; industries per ZDNet: chemicals, shipbuilding, display, home appliances, logistics, medical, hospitality — automotive/steel/battery explicitly *not yet finalized*). The National Growth Fund (국민성장펀드, via the Financial Services Commission) adds **₩16T in 2026** across six physical-AI-adjacent sectors (AI, robots, future vehicles, defense, semiconductors, batteries) under the M.AX Frontier Project (announced 2026-07-01).
- The "Hyundai $5.8B" reported by Ars = Hyundai Motor Group's **₩9T Saemangeum (Jeonbuk) robot-AI-hydrogen hub**, a commitment formalized earlier in 2026 (investment agreement with the state and Jeonbuk province 2026-02-27; financing MOU with four policy-finance institutions — KDB, IBK, Korea Eximbank, KODIT — signed 2026-04-06 as the KDB policy-finance council's "No. 1 project") and folded into the physical-AI axis: AI data center ₩5.8T (~50k GPUs, build 2027→2029), **robot manufacturing + parts cluster ₩0.4T (30,000 robots/yr plant, build 2028→2029)**, renewables ₩1.3T, electrolyser plant ₩1T, hydrogen-AI demo city ₩0.4T. Note: this Korean 30k/yr plant is **distinct from the US RMAC** (below) despite the matching capacity figure, and only ~4% of the $5.8B is robot manufacturing per se.
- Samsung exercised its call option (announced 2024-12-31, completed 2025-02) to raise its stake in **Rainbow Robotics from 14.7% to ~35% for KRW 267B (~$181.6M)**, becoming controlling shareholder and consolidating it as a subsidiary. A **Future Robotics Office** reporting to the CEO's office was created, led by Jun-ho Oh (KAIST HUBO creator; Rainbow co-founder).
- Rainbow Robotics (founded 2011 out of KAIST's HUBO lab) sells the **RB-Y1**: dual 7-DoF arms on a fast wheeled base, ~140 cm tall — a wheeled-humanoid research/industrial platform.
- **2026-03**: Samsung announced plans to convert all global production facilities into "AI-driven factories" by **2030**, deploying RB-Y1 on its own lines with agentic AI across warehousing-to-shipment.
- **2026-06**: RB-Y1 reported to have entered a proof-of-concept at a **Coupang warehouse** — Korea's first commercial humanoid warehouse trial; larger order contingent on results (ETNews report 2026-06-14, also carried by Korea Herald; none of the three companies has publicly confirmed the pilot).

### LG Electronics
- **2025-01**: acquired majority control (51%) of Bear Robotics (Silicon Valley service-robot maker) via call option; Bear becomes LG's commercial service-robot arm alongside industrial subsidiary RoboStar.
- **CES 2026 (2026-01)**: unveiled **CLOiD**, a home humanoid for the "Zero Labor Home" — 7-DoF arms, five-fingered hands, built on LG's "Affectionate Intelligence" stack.
- **2026-06/07**: established a CEO-level **Robotics Business Center**, signaling a shift from R&D to commercialization across home, commercial and industrial robots plus components (actuators) and a robot-data factory.

### Hyundai Motor Group / Boston Dynamics
- **2026-06**: Hyundai agreed to buy SoftBank's remaining **9.65% of Boston Dynamics for $325M** (put option from the 2021 deal), taking **100% ownership**.
- **CES 2026 strategy**: Robot Metaplant Application Center (**RMAC**) opens 2026 in the US; robot manufacturing capacity of **30,000 units/year by 2028**; Atlas deployed at Hyundai Metaplant America (Georgia) from **2028** (parts sequencing), expanding to assembly by **2030**. Funded within Hyundai's **$26B US investment (2025–2028)** and **KRW 125.2T Korea investment over five years from 2026**.
- Atlas's entire 2026 production run is committed to RMAC and **Google DeepMind** (Gemini Robotics on Atlas) — Boston Dynamics itself sits in [Landscape: USA](landscape-usa.md), but its owner, capital and factory roadmap are Korean.

### Doosan Robotics & the K-Humanoid Alliance
- Doosan Robotics (Korea's #1 cobot maker) declared a 2025 pivot from hardware-centric cobots to "practical humanoids": AI/SW and humanoid R&D orgs stood up in H2 2025, Innovation Center in Bundang opened 2025-09, US integrator ONExia acquired 2025-07, and a physical-AI collaboration with NVIDIA (announced 2025/2026, incl. AI-factory infrastructure across Doosan Group).
- **K-Humanoid Alliance** (launched **2025-04-10** by MOTIE at the Plaza Hotel, Seoul; primary-verified): ~40 organizations at launch (robot makers, parts suppliers, AI labs, demand-side companies; later coverage counts 50+), with **combined public-private investment of over KRW 1 trillion by 2030** across R&D, fund formation and M&A, toward making Korea a top humanoid nation by 2030; Doosan, Rainbow and academic labs participate. Korea's edge: actuators, batteries, semiconductors and chaebol factories as first customers.
- Separately, MSIT (과기정통부) launched a **"K-AI humanoid" public-private development consortium (2026-05-18)**: KIST-led, with LG Electronics, LG AI Research, LG Energy Solution, SNU, KAIST and Korea University — but at only **₩50.4B through 2030**; do not conflate with the K-Humanoid Alliance or the mega-project figures above.

## Rest of world

### Israel
- **Mentee Robotics** (Herzliya, founded 2022 by Amnon Shashua): MenteeBot full-size humanoid (~1.75 m, 25 kg payload, swappable battery >3 h in V3, unveiled 2025-02); serial production for logistics customers began mid-2025.
- **Mobileye agreed to acquire Mentee for $900M (announced 2026-01-06** at CES; $611.9M cash + 26,279,824 Mobileye Class A shares issued at closing; closed **2026-02-03**), folding humanoids into Mobileye's "Physical AI" strategy while running Mentee as an independent unit. Series production/commercialization targeted for 2028. Shashua chairs both companies and recused himself from the board vote.

### India
- India ranks #3 globally in number of humanoid-robotics startups (~14, vs 28 US / 26 China, per Business Standard, as of 2025-12) — mostly local software + globally sourced hardware.
- Early-stage robotics funding surged: >$45M in H1 2026, ~+120% YoY (aggregator estimate, unverified). Examples: iHub Robotics (Kerala; Tara Gen-1 semi-humanoid exported to UAE/Saudi; building a humanoid plant), Muks Robotics (Pune, bootstrapped humanoids).
- India's bigger role may be as a **manufacturing base**: NEURA Robotics plans production scale-up across Germany *and India* (as of 2026-06).

### UAE / Gulf
- Abu Dhabi is positioning as a deployment sandbox: a full-size humanoid (from China's LimX Dynamics) is slated for 2026 pilots in Abu Dhabi malls/facilities after its BRIDGE Summit 2025 showcase.
- Gulf sovereign capital increasingly appears on humanoid cap tables (e.g., via funds and strategic LPs); iHub's Tara robots already operate in UAE hospitality (single-source, unverified). See [Investment](investment.md).

### Elsewhere
- **Canada**: Sanctuary AI (Vancouver) continues dexterity-focused work (Phoenix humanoid, hydraulic hands) but has shifted away from a full-humanoid consumer narrative after 2024–25 leadership changes (unverified).
- **Norway–US, UK, Switzerland**: strong robot-learning academia (ETH Zurich's ANYmal lineage → US spinouts; Oxford/Imperial → UK startups) feeds talent into the companies above; see [Key people](key-people.md) and [Organizations](organizations.md).

## Open questions
- Can NEURA convert an up-to-$1.4B round (tranched, condition-dependent) into actual 4NE1 volume shipments by late 2026? Tranche conditions are undisclosed.
- Does Japan's consortium model (slow, state-funded, incumbent-led) produce a competitive robot foundation model before US/Chinese [VLA models](vla-models.md) commoditize the layer?
- Korea's humanoid AI is heavily partnered (DeepMind for Atlas); whether Samsung/LG build sovereign robot-AI stacks is a key 2026–27 watch item.
- Whether 1X's home-first strategy survives contact with reliability/teleop-privacy economics — its 2025-12 industrial pivot deal suggests hedging. See [Open problems](open-problems.md).
