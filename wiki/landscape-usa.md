> The USA is the center of gravity of Physical AI capital and software: as of 2026-07 it hosts the two most highly valued humanoid makers (Figure at $39B, Tesla's Optimus program), the two most highly valued robot-brain labs (Skild AI at $14B+, Physical Intelligence at ~$5.6B and reportedly raising at $11B), the dominant platform vendor (NVIDIA), and all of the big-tech embodied-AI efforts (Google DeepMind's Gemini Robotics, Meta's Robotics Studio, OpenAI's new robotics division). 2026 milestones include the first US humanoid IPO path (Agility's $2.5B SPAC), Boston Dynamics' production electric Atlas, 1X's consumer NEO shipping from a Hayward factory, and robotics venture funding running ahead of all of 2025 by mid-year. Canada's footprint is smaller — Sanctuary AI in Vancouver plus deployment sites and academic labs. See [Landscape: China](landscape-china.md) for the main rival ecosystem and [Investment](investment.md) for cross-cutting funding data.

## Humanoid hardware companies

- **Figure** — raised >$1B Series C at a **$39B post-money valuation** (announced 2025-09-16; lead Parkway Venture Capital, with Brookfield, NVIDIA, Macquarie, Intel Capital, LG Technology Ventures, Salesforce, T-Mobile Ventures, Qualcomm Ventures). Ended its OpenAI partnership in early 2025 and built the in-house **Helix** VLA. Unveiled **Figure 03** (2025-10), redesigned for mass manufacture and home settings; **BotQ** factory targets ~100,000 robots over four years (company target, as of 2025-10). Deployment proof point: Figure 02 ran daily shifts at BMW Spartanburg for ~11 months, loading 90,000+ parts into production of 30,000+ X3s; ~40 Figure 03 units reported at the same plant (unverified). See [Humanoid robots](humanoid-robots.md).
- **Tesla Optimus** — Tesla disclosed producing "several hundred" Optimus units in Q1 2026 (learning/data-collection phase). Converted the Fremont Model S/X lines (last S/X built 2026-05) to Optimus; Musk guided **V3 production start late July–August 2026**, initial output "quite slow" (~10,000 unique parts), with mass production targeted late 2026 and a **1M units/year design capacity** at Fremont; a second line at Giga Texas is planned with up to 10M/year long-term design capacity (company statements — treat all Tesla timelines as aspirational; prior Optimus dates have slipped). Stated long-run price target $20–30K (unverified).
- **Boston Dynamics** (Hyundai Motor Group ~80%+ owner; SoftBank reportedly exited its remaining stake for ~$325M (unverified)) — unveiled the **production version of the electric Atlas at CES on 2026-01-05**; reported specs: 56 DoF, ~2.3 m reach, ~50 kg lift (press reporting, unverified against spec sheet). The **entire 2026 Atlas production run is committed to two customers: Hyundai's Robotics Metaplant Application Center (Georgia) and Google DeepMind**. Hyundai's $26B US investment plan includes a robot factory sized for ~30,000 robots/year; Hyundai Mobis supplies actuators.
- **Agility Robotics** — announced on 2026-06-24 a **SPAC merger with Churchill Capital Corp XI at ~$2.5B pre-money valuation**, >$620M gross proceeds ($420M Churchill XI trust + ~$200M PIPE led by Foxconn), ticker AGLT on a North American exchange not yet named (Churchill XI itself trades on Nasdaq as CCXI), expected to close in 2026 — set to be the first US humanoid maker to go public. Per its filings: Digit operates at **9 customer facilities with 65,000+ cumulative operating hours**; customers include GXO Logistics, Schaeffler, Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada, and Mercado Libre; **>$300M in multi-year Digit v5 orders** (milestone-contingent). Digit v5 is pitched as the first "cooperatively safe" humanoid (company claim).
- **Apptronik** — closed a **$520M Series A extension on 2026-02-11** (total Series A >$935M; total raised ~$1B; ~$5B valuation per CNBC). Backers: B Capital, Google, Mercedes-Benz, PEAK6, plus new investors AT&T Ventures, John Deere, Qatar Investment Authority. Apollo pilots with Mercedes-Benz, GXO, Jabil. Unveiled **Apollo 2** on 2026-06-30 alongside "Robot Park," a 90,000 sq ft data-collection and training campus in Austin where Apollo 2 fleets continuously gather real-world manipulation data for models developed with Google DeepMind (as of 2026-07). Spun out of UT Austin's Human Centered Robotics Lab.
- **1X Technologies** (Norwegian-founded, US-headquartered; OpenAI-backed in earlier rounds) — opened NEO pre-orders 2025-10 at **$20,000 purchase or $499/month subscription**; company claims ~10,000 pre-orders in the first five days (unverified). **Full-scale NEO production began 2026-04 at its 58,000 sq ft Hayward, CA factory** (200+ staff), first deliveries to US homes promised before end-2026. NEO runs 1X's **Redwood** generalist model on NVIDIA Jetson Thor; early autonomy is supplemented by human teleoperation, which drew privacy scrutiny.

## Robot-brain labs (models, not bodies)

- **Physical Intelligence (π)** — founded 2024 (Sergey Levine, Karol Hausman, Chelsea Finn et al.; Berkeley/Stanford/Google-DeepMind roots). Model line: **π0** (2024-10), **π0-FAST + open π0 weights** (2025-02), **π0.5** open-world mobile manipulation (2025-04), **π*0.6 trained with the RECAP RL method** (2025-11-17; claimed ~2x throughput and ~half the failure rate on hardest tasks; espresso service ran 5:30am-11:30pm ≈ 18h per PI's blog — see [PI deep dive](company-pi.md)), **π0.7** "steerable" foundation model (2026-04-16). Funding: $400M Series A @ $2.4B (2024-11), **$600M Series B @ ~$5.6B (2025-11, CapitalG + Lux lead)**; a ~$1B round at **$11B valuation** reported in progress as of 2026-H1 (press reports, unverified). Investors include Jeff Bezos, OpenAI, Sequoia, Thrive. See [VLA models](vla-models.md).
- **Skild AI** — Pittsburgh; founded by CMU professors Deepak Pathak and Abhinav Gupta. **$1.4B Series C at >$14B valuation (2026-01-14)**, led by SoftBank with NVentures (NVIDIA), Bezos Expeditions, Macquarie Capital, 1789 Capital, and strategics LG, Schneider Electric, CommonSpirit, Salesforce Ventures (Samsung joined the prior ~$4.5B round in mid-2025 with ~$10M, not the Series C). Sells **Skild Brain**, pitched as an "omni-bodied" model controlling quadrupeds, humanoids, arms, and mobile manipulators; deployments claimed in security, construction, data centers, warehouses; company claims revenue went 0 → ~$30M during 2025 (company figure, unverified).
- Adjacent US model labs: **World Labs** (Fei-Fei Li; spatial/world models — see [World models](world-models.md)) and Toyota Research Institute's large behavior models work (unverified currency).

## NVIDIA's platform play

NVIDIA is positioning as the "Android of generalist robotics" (TechCrunch framing, 2026-01) — supplying compute, simulation, world models, and pretrained robot policies while investing in customers such as Figure (Series B/C) and Skild AI (via NVentures), and supplying onboard compute to 1X, Physical Intelligence partners, and most other US players.

- **GTC 2026-03-16 announcements**: **Cosmos 3** world foundation model (unifying synthetic world generation, vision reasoning, action simulation); **GR00T N1.7** robot foundation model in early access with commercial licensing; **GR00T N2** previewed for end-2026; **Isaac Lab 3.0**; **Newton physics engine 1.0** (with Google DeepMind/Disney lineage). Partners named include Figure, Agility, Boston Dynamics, Skild AI, 1X, FANUC, ABB, KUKA, Yaskawa. See [Simulation](simulation.md) and [World models](world-models.md).
- **Jetson Thor** (2025) is the de facto onboard compute for US humanoids (used by 1X NEO, Figure-class robots); the **Jetson AGX Thor T5000** offers ~2,070 FP4 TFLOPS, 128GB unified memory, 40–130W (as of 2026-05 spec).
- **Isaac GR00T reference humanoid** (announced 2026-05-31, GTC Taipei): open reference design pairing a **Unitree H2 Plus** body (a Chinese platform — notable cross-Pacific dependency) with Sharpa hands, Jetson Thor, and the Isaac GR00T stack; launch users Ai2, ETH Zurich, Stanford Robotics Center, UCSD; available from Unitree late 2026. See [Hardware](hardware.md).

## Big tech

- **Google DeepMind** — the most complete big-tech embodied stack: **Gemini Robotics** VLA (2025-03), **On-Device** variant (2025-06), **Gemini Robotics 1.5 + ER 1.5** with think-before-act reasoning and cross-embodiment motion transfer (2025-09). Hardware-agnostic partner strategy: deep Apptronik partnership (Apollo), trusted testers Agility, Boston Dynamics, Agile Robots, Enchanted Tools — and it is one of the two buyers of the entire 2026 electric Atlas run, signaling a scaled Gemini-on-Atlas research program. Alphabet also invests via CapitalG (Physical Intelligence lead) and Google (Apptronik).
- **Meta** — building a platform play inside **Meta Superintelligence Labs**: a "Meta Robotics Studio" team (launched 2025) plus the **acquisition of Assured Robot Intelligence (2026-05)**, bringing academics Lerrel Pinto (NYU) and Xiaolong Wang (UCSD). Stated strategy: develop sensors, software, and robot AI models licensed to third-party humanoid makers ("Android model") rather than sell robots (as of 2026-05 reporting).
- **OpenAI** — re-entered robotics after its 2025 Figure breakup: a dedicated **robotics division** announced targeting humanoids for infrastructure work and eventually a personal robot; ~11 SF hardware/sim/data/ML roles open as of 2026-06; Aditya Ramesh (DALL-E/Sora creator, VP of Research leading Worldsim) states on his personal site that he is bootstrapping OpenAI's new robotics effort (as of 2026); robotics hardware lead Caitlin Kalinowski reportedly resigned 2026-03-07 over Pentagon-deal / autonomy-guardrail concerns (multiple secondary reports, no OpenAI confirmation). OpenAI remains an investor in Physical Intelligence and 1X.
- **Amazon** — a deployment gravity well rather than a model lab: named among Digit deployment customers, and runs its own warehouse-robotics AI programs (see [Organizations](organizations.md)).
- **Tesla** — vertically integrated: own hardware, own FSD-derived neural nets, own factories as both training ground and first customer (see above).

## Academic hubs and open source

- **Stanford** — REAL (Shuran Song), IRIS (Chelsea Finn → PI co-founder), Stanford Robotics Center (GR00T reference-robot launch user); co-origin of **OpenVLA** (7B open VLA trained on 970k demos; outperformed 55B RT-2-X by ~16.5 pts absolute success across 29 tasks at release).
- **UC Berkeley** — BAIR / Robot Learning Lab (Pieter Abbeel, Sergey Levine → PI); persistent source of RL-for-robotics talent.
- **CMU (Pittsburgh)** — Robotics Institute; direct pipeline into Skild AI; anchors the Pittsburgh robotics cluster.
- **Others** — MIT, UCSD (Xiaolong Wang → Meta), NYU (Lerrel Pinto → Meta), UT Austin (→ Apptronik), Ai2 in Seattle (GR00T reference-robot launch user). Hugging Face's **LeRobot** ecosystem (SmolVLA, 450M params, community datasets) is the main open on-ramp; see [Data](data.md) and [Key people](key-people.md).

## Canada

- **Sanctuary AI** (Vancouver, BC) — the anchor Canadian player. Phoenix humanoid with hydraulic dexterous hands + Carbon control stack. Turbulent 2024–25: CTO Suzanne Gildert left 2024-04, CEO Geordie Rose removed by the board 2024-11, James Wells (ex-CCO) as CEO; pivoted toward industrial deployments and selling teleoperation/dexterity software for third-party platforms. Hiring again as of 2026-01 (~19 open roles, deployment-focused) and claims Phoenix deployed in real manufacturing plants (company claims, largely unverified). Notable patent portfolio in humanoid robotics (IAM analysis).
- **Deployment and supply footprint** — Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada is a named Agility Digit customer site; Ontario's automation corridor (ex-Clearpath/OTTO Motors, acquired by Rockwell 2023) supplies mobile-robot expertise (context, as of 2023).
- **Academics** — University of Toronto Robotics Institute / Vector Institute and Mila contribute RL and robot-learning research but no Canadian robot-foundation-model lab at US scale exists as of 2026-07 (assessment, unverified).

## Funding climate (as of 2026-07)

- Robotics startups raised **$18.8B globally in 2026 through 2026-06-22 (Crunchbase basis) — already exceeding Crunchbase's ~$15B total for all of 2025** and its 2021 peak (~$14.1B); a broader tally (Briefs) puts 2026 YTD at >$23B vs ~$26B for all of 2025. Figures vary widely by source and definition — treat absolute totals as indicative. See [Investment](investment.md).
- US megadeals dominate: Figure $1B+ (2025-09), Physical Intelligence $600M (2025-11), Skild $1.4B (2026-01), Apptronik $520M (2026-02), plus the reported PI raise at $11B (unverified).
- Character of the market: valuations price in a general-purpose future while revenues are tiny ($30M-scale at best); SoftBank, sovereign funds (QIA), Bezos, and corporate strategics (NVIDIA, Google, Samsung, LG, Hyundai, Mercedes, John Deere) are the marginal buyers; public-market access is opening via Agility's SPAC.
- Twelve commercial humanoid platforms were purchasable or leasable in early 2026 vs ~3 in 2024 (SVRC industry report, unverified). US strength is capital + models + chips; scaled low-cost manufacturing remains the comparative weakness vs [China](landscape-china.md).
